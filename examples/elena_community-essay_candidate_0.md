# Tomorrow's Lesson — Elena (Grade 7)

## Part A — Teacher Cheat Sheet

### Before the bell
- [[NEW]]Put Elena’s visual schedule on her desk with 4 boxes: Intro Slide Deck → During Reading Questions → Independent Practice → Partner Discussion.[[/NEW]]  
- [[NEW]]Place the modified student packet face-up at her seat and open to page 1 of the graphic organizer, plus a sticky note that says: “clue → guess → check.”[[/NEW]]  
- [[NEW]]Seat Elena away from the door, pencil sharpener, and brightest fluorescent light; set her noise-cancelling headphones on the desk without comment.[[/NEW]]

### Watch for
- [[NEW]]She starts answering literally instead of inferentially, especially on phrases like “identity-forming narrative,” “slighted,” or “community has a strange power.”[[/NEW]]
- [[NEW]]She stops writing, scripts the text, or looks toward the door during the paragraph about “shared story” or the long bulleted lists in paragraphs 2 and 8.[[/NEW]]
- [[NEW]]She gives very short, flat responses during partner talk or begins to withdraw when the lesson asks her to talk about a community she belongs to.[[/NEW]]

### If they disengage
1. [[NEW]]Point to the visual schedule and say, “You’re on During Reading Questions. Use your ‘clue → guess → check’ card for the next question.”[[/NEW]]
2. [[NEW]]If she still stalls, say, “You can use headphones or take your 3-minute safe-space pass. Come back and answer just one question with the frame.”[[/NEW]]

### Praise specifically
- [[NEW]]“You used the text clue and didn’t guess from memory — that is exactly how strong readers handle inference.”[[/NEW]]
- [[NEW]]“You found evidence in paragraph __ and matched it to the question. That’s precise reading.”[[/NEW]]
- [[NEW]]“You gave a partner answer with a clear academic sentence. That was strong, organized communication.”[[/NEW]]

## Part B — Modified Lesson Plan

### 1) Intro Slide Deck — 5 min

- **Original step:** Guide students through the slide deck to introduce them to the reading lesson and skill focus.
- **Modified step for Elena (Grade 7):** [[CHANGED]]Keep Elena in whole group for the intro, but hand her the visual schedule and a 1-line purpose card before you start: “Today we are finding the central idea and the details that prove it.” Pause after the lesson purpose and ask her to underline “central idea” and “details” on her copy.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** Goal 3 — ELA — Figurative language and inference: “Identify character motivation using a 3-step text-evidence frame, 4/5 prompts.” Also, present levels: “With sentence-frame organizer, accuracy rises to 75–85% on inference items.”
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Purpose card, visual schedule, and a 2-word highlight task on her packet.[[/NEW]]

### 2) Vocabulary setup — embedded in Intro Slide Deck

- **Original step:** Let’s pronounce these words together as a class: Aspect, Moral, Narrative, Specific.
- **Modified step for Elena (Grade 7):** [[CHANGED]]Before the class reads, give Elena a tiny word bank with student-friendly meanings for “narrative,” “specific,” and “aspect.” After the class pronunciation, have her match each word to a short meaning on the page instead of repeating the class chant.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** Goal 3 — ELA — Figurative language and inference: “Pre-teach figurative language and idioms before any text containing them.” Present level: “Literal comprehension is strong; figurative language, inference, and perspective-taking are emerging.”
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]3-word vocabulary match strip with simple definitions.[[/NEW]]

### 3) Whole Class Reading — Paragraphs 1–2 — 15 min

- **Original step:** Read paragraphs 1–2 and answer During Reading Questions A, B, C.
- **Modified step for Elena (Grade 7):** [[CHANGED]]Read paragraphs 1–2 with the class. Before Question A, give Elena 30 seconds of quiet think-time and point to her organizer box labeled “clue.” For Question B, let her answer by circling one sentence from paragraph 2 that best matches the claim, then complete the frame orally or in writing: “The claim says ____. The list shows ____.” Keep her in the whole-group setting.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** Goal 3 — ELA — Figurative language and inference: “No ‘cold call’ on inference questions without 30-second think-time and access to organizer.” Accommodations: “Sentence-frame organizers for inference and figurative-language items (‘The text says ___ → I think this means ___ → because ___’).” Placement: “Full Inclusion Program.”
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Sentence-frame organizer + circling one proof sentence instead of open-ended verbal explaining.[[/NEW]]

### 4) Whole Class Reading — Paragraphs 3–7 — 15 min

- **Original step:** Partner reading and During Reading Question A and B.
- **Modified step for Elena (Grade 7):** [[CHANGED]]Keep the partner reading, but assign Elena a clear role: she reads only the first sentence of paragraph 3 aloud, then her partner reads the next chunk. After paragraph 5 or 6, pause and ask her to fill in one organizer line: “The author says he belongs to Newcastle because ____.” If the class does Turn & Talk, let her use a written response instead of speaking, then share only if she volunteers.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** Accommodations: “Assigned role in every group task (recorder, timekeeper, materials manager)” and “Written response accepted in place of oral participation for whole-group share-outs.” Present level: “Prefers parallel play and structured cooperative tasks with assigned roles.”
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Role card + one-line written response box for the turn-and-talk.[[/NEW]]

### 5) Whole Class Reading — Paragraph 8 — 5 min

- **Original step:** Read paragraph 8 and answer During Reading Question A and B.
- **Modified step for Elena (Grade 7):** [[CHANGED]]Before paragraph 8, preview that the author is “testing” his definition against a list. After reading, give Elena a checklist with the six test points reduced to short labels: types, feelings, identity, moral, different from other groups, many communities. Have her match 2 of the labels to lines in paragraph 8 instead of answering all of them orally.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** Goal 3 — ELA — Figurative language and inference: benchmark “Distinguish literal vs figurative meaning of a target sentence in a grade-level passage, 4/5 items.” Present level: “Strong pattern recognition, precise vocabulary, strong work ethic when expectations are explicit and visual.”
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Six-point checklist with short labels + line-match task.[[/NEW]]

### 6) Whole Class Reading — Paragraphs 9–11 — 5 min

- **Original step:** Find evidence that answers the title question.
- **Modified step for Elena (Grade 7):** [[CHANGED]]Tell Elena exactly what to hunt for: one sentence that defines community and one sentence that explains why it matters. She highlights both, then writes the paragraph number next to each highlight. If she finishes early, ask her to explain which one is the definition and which one is the “why it matters” sentence.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** Goal 3 — ELA — Figurative language and inference: “Generate one inference per paragraph during guided reading, with text evidence cited.” Accommodations: “Directions given in writing AND orally; no idioms in directions.”
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Two-highlight evidence hunt with paragraph-number boxes.[[/NEW]]

### 7) Independent Practice — MCQ 1–4 — 5 min

- **Original step:** Answer multiple choice questions.
- **Modified step for Elena (Grade 7):** [[CHANGED]]Give Elena the modified MCQ sheet with a one-line “what this question is really asking” under each item. Keep her in the room, let her use headphones, and require her to mark one text clue before choosing an answer for each question.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** Goal 3 — ELA — Figurative language and inference: “With sentence-frame organizer, accuracy rises to 75–85% on inference items.” Accommodation: “Noise-cancelling headphones permitted at all times without staff prompting.”
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Modified MCQ page with gloss lines and clue-marking.[[/NEW]]

### 8) Independent Practice — Short Response — 15 min

- **Original step:** Explain what Lowe means when he says a community is “a group of people who share an identity-forming narrative.”
- **Modified step for Elena (Grade 7):** [[CHANGED]]Hand Elena the sentence-stem scaffold at the start of Independent Practice, not after she gets stuck. Ask her to answer in 4–6 sentences using the organizer: define the phrase, give 2 details from the text, and use at least 1 unit word from the word bank. She may type the response instead of handwriting if needed.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** Goal 3 — ELA — Figurative language and inference: benchmark “Distinguish literal vs figurative meaning of a target sentence in a grade-level passage, 4/5 items” and “Generate one inference per paragraph during guided reading, with text evidence cited.” Accommodation: “Permission to type rather than handwrite when fine-motor fatigue triggers regulation issues.”
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Sentence stems, checklist, and optional typing.[[/NEW]]

### 9) Student-Led Discussion — 5 min

- **Original step:** Discuss questions with a partner and record both answers.
- **Modified step for Elena (Grade 7):** [[CHANGED]]Pair Elena with one familiar, calm partner. Give her a role card: “Speaker 1 / Recorder.” She answers question 1 in writing first, then reads her own answer to the partner if she chooses. For question 2, prompt her to use the text: “A person can belong to a community without agreeing with every view because ____.” For question 3, let her point to or circle benefits before speaking.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** Goal 1 — Pragmatic communication / Self-regulation: “use a taught repair phrase (‘Wait, did you mean that literally?’, ‘I need a minute’)” and present level: “Prefers parallel play and structured cooperative tasks with assigned roles.” Accommodation: “Written response accepted in place of oral participation for whole-group share-outs.”
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Partner role card + discussion frame + written-first response box.[[/NEW]]
