# Tomorrow's Lesson — Jasmine (Grade 7)

## PART A — Teacher Cheat Sheet

### Before the bell
- [[NEW]]Put Jasmine’s modified packet on her desk before class starts: the question sheet, short-response scaffold, and the RI.7.2 graphic organizer.[[/NEW]]  
  [[NEW]]Why: she has attention/task-initiation needs and benefits from a copy of the teacher’s notes, checklists, and graphic organizers — “Repeat directions,” “Copy of teacher’s notes,” and “Reference sheets, graphic organizers, and checklists.”[[/NEW]]

- [[NEW]]Seat Jasmine in front, near you, with her packet open to page 1 and a highlighter/pencil ready.[[/NEW]]  
  [[NEW]]Why: her accommodations say “Sit in the front of the room,” and she tends to shut down when frustrated, so starting with materials already out lowers the chance she stalls before the text starts.[[/NEW]]

- [[NEW]]Write these 3 board cues where she can see them during the read: “Pause. Plan. Proceed.” “Track the text.” “If stuck: breathe, then ask.”[[/NEW]]  
  [[NEW]]Why: her IEP explicitly names “Reminders to pause, plan, proceed” and Goal 1 — Counseling / Self-regulation says she needs to “use a taught calming strategy…to calm and re-engage.”[[/NEW]]

### Watch for
- [[NEW]]She stops tracking the text during the long first paragraph and looks down at her page without marking anything.[[/NEW]]  
  [[NEW]]Why this matters today: the lesson asks students to identify the central idea and details, and her ELA present level says she “needs frequent reminders to track text, annotate, reread when independent.”[[/NEW]]

- [[NEW]]She answers the first question with a very short, vague response like “it means people are together” and then goes quiet.[[/NEW]]  
  [[NEW]]Why this matters today: Goal 3 — ELA says she needs to “answer literal and inferential comprehension questions” and she struggles with “literal/inferential questions verbally and in writing.”[[/NEW]]

- [[NEW]]She starts asking to leave the room, puts her head down, or stops writing during Independent Practice.[[/NEW]]  
  [[NEW]]Why this matters today: her ELA present level says when frustrated she “disengages by putting head down or asking to use restroom multiple times,” and her behavior profile says “Frustration → withdrawal.”[[/NEW]]

### If they disengage
- [[NEW]]Step 1: Point to the board cue and say, “Pause. Plan. Proceed. Take one slow breath with me.”[[/NEW]]  
  [[NEW]]Why: this matches the IEP accommodation “Reminders to pause, plan, proceed” and Goal 1’s expectation to use a taught calming strategy.[[/NEW]]

- [[NEW]]Step 2: Hand her the one-line support from the packet and say, “Read just this sentence, then answer with the stem.” Stay with her until she writes one sentence.[[/NEW]]  
  [[NEW]]Why: Goal 3 — ELA says she should “independently answer literal and inferential comprehension questions,” and the packet gives her a sentence stem plus a tiny first step so she can re-enter the task without leaving whole group.[[/NEW]]

### Praise specifically
- [[NEW]]“Jasmine, you used the text to explain the author’s idea — that is exactly what RI.7.2 asks for.”[[/NEW]]

- [[NEW]]“I noticed you paused, reset, and came back to the question instead of shutting down. That is strong self-regulation.”[[/NEW]]

- [[NEW]]“You found a detail from paragraph 7 and connected it to the central idea. That’s the kind of evidence-based thinking strong readers do.”[[/NEW]]

## PART B — Modified Lesson Plan

### 1) Intro Slide Deck — 5 min
- **Original step:** Guide students through the slide deck to introduce them to the reading lesson and skill focus.
- **Modified step for Jasmine:** [[CHANGED]]Keep her in whole group, but give her a one-page “listen for” card before the slides begin. As you introduce the skill, ask her to underline the words “central idea” and “supporting details” on the card, then point to the text title and purpose line. Do not pull her out.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[NEW]]Goal 3 — ELA: “Independently answer literal and inferential comprehension questions or writing prompts that meet grade-level rubrics”; present level says she “needs frequent reminders to track text, annotate, reread when independent.”[[/NEW]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Listen-for card with 2 blanks: “This text is mostly about ___.” “Details will help prove ___.”[[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[NEW]]Right as you open the lesson and before the first slide explains the purpose.[[/NEW]]

### 2) Vocabulary / text setup — built into intro
- **Original step:** Pronounce and preview vocabulary with the class.
- **Modified step for Jasmine:** [[CHANGED]]Say each vocabulary word once, then give Jasmine a tiny margin box with the four words and a student-friendly meaning next to each. Ask her to circle the word “narrative” because it will matter most later.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[NEW]]Accommodation: “Repeat directions”; present level: “Struggles with literal/inferential questions verbally and in writing” and needs help with “vocabulary.”[[/NEW]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Mini-vocabulary box in her packet: aspect = part; moral = right/wrong; narrative = story; specific = exact.[[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[NEW]]During the vocabulary preview on the intro slide deck.[[/NEW]]

### 3) Whole Class Reading — paragraphs 1–2 — 15 min
- **Original step:** Students read and answer the During Reading Questions.
- **Modified step for Jasmine:** [[CHANGED]]Keep her in the whole-class read. Before paragraph 1, hand her a highlighter and ask her to mark only two places: one sentence that sounds like the main idea and one bullet in paragraph 2 that gives a key trait. After each teacher pause, have her whisper-read the answer to you or a partner before writing one short note in the packet.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[NEW]]Accommodation: “Copy of teacher’s notes,” “Reference sheets, graphic organizers, and checklists,” and “1:1 check-ins”; present level says she “benefits immensely from small group / 1:1, but chooses whole group 80% of the time when small group offered.”[[/NEW]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]A 2-column note strip: “Claim about community” / “Detail that proves it.”[[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[NEW]]At the first pause after paragraph 1 and again after paragraph 2.[[/NEW]]

#### During Reading Questions — Paragraphs 1–2
- **Original question A:** What claim does Lowe make about the word “community” in paragraph 1?
- **Modified question A for Jasmine:** [[CHANGED]]What is Lowe saying about the word “community” in paragraph 1? Choose one idea and explain it in a short phrase.[[/CHANGED]]  
  [[NEW]]What this question is really asking: “What does the author think is true about the word ‘community’?”[[/NEW]]

- **Original question B:** What is the relationship between the claim above and the bulleted list in paragraph 2?
- **Modified question B for Jasmine:** [[CHANGED]]How does the bullet list in paragraph 2 help explain the claim in paragraph 1? Use the words “claim” and “detail.”[[/CHANGED]]  
  [[NEW]]What this question is really asking: “How do the bullets prove or explain the main idea?”[[/NEW]]

- **Original question C:** Based on the bulleted list, summarize at least three key traits of a community.
- **Modified question C for Jasmine:** [[CHANGED]]Name 3 traits of a community from the bullets. Write them as short phrases, not full sentences.[[/CHANGED]]  
  [[NEW]]What this question is really asking: “What are three important characteristics the author says community has?”[[/NEW]]

### 4) Partner Reading — paragraphs 3–7 — 10 min
- **Original step:** Students read in partners and answer the During Reading Question.
- **Modified step for Jasmine:** [[CHANGED]]Let Jasmine stay with a partner in the main room. Give her the paragraph 5–7 section with one bracket already marked at paragraph 7. After partner reading, have her point to one sentence that shows how Newcastle fits Lowe’s definition before she speaks.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[NEW]]Goal 3 — ELA benchmark: “Accurately annotate a given text for the prompt — labeled annotation focus, supporting detail, etc.” and accommodation “Repeat directions.”[[/NEW]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]One-page partner-reading slip with a single box: “This sentence shows community is a shared story because ___.”[[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[NEW]]Right when partner reading starts, after paragraph 2 and before the first partner turn.[[/NEW]]

#### During Reading Question — Paragraphs 3–7
- **Original question A:** How does Lowe’s example of belonging to the Newcastle community support his definition?
- **Modified question A for Jasmine:** [[CHANGED]]How does the Newcastle example help prove Lowe’s definition of community? Give one detail from paragraphs 5–7.[[/CHANGED]]  
  [[NEW]]What this question is really asking: “How does this example show the author’s idea in action?”[[/NEW]]

- **Original question B:** Consider the communities you are a part of. Share how one of these communities fits Lowe’s definition.
- **Modified question B for Jasmine:** [[CHANGED]]Think of one community you belong to. Tell how it fits Lowe’s definition using one sentence stem: “I am part of ___ because ___.”[[/CHANGED]]  
  [[NEW]]What this question is really asking: “How does your own experience connect to the author’s definition?”[[/NEW]]

### 5) Whole Class Reading — paragraph 8 — 5 min
- **Original step:** Students read and answer the During Reading Questions.
- **Modified step for Jasmine:** [[CHANGED]]Before paragraph 8, ask Jasmine to number the six bullets 1–6 in the margin. After the read, have her match one earlier bullet from paragraph 2 to one later bullet in paragraph 8 by drawing a line between them.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[NEW]]Goal 3 — ELA benchmark: “Independently find effective pieces of textual evidence to support the claim.”[[/NEW]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]A numbered list box with two columns: “Paragraph 2 criterion” and “Paragraph 8 test.”[[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[NEW]]During the paragraph 8 reread, right before the class discusses why Lowe adds another list.[[/NEW]]

#### During Reading Question — Paragraph 8
- **Original question A:** Why does the author include another bulleted list in this section?
- **Modified question A for Jasmine:** [[CHANGED]]Why does Lowe add a second bullet list in paragraph 8? Use the words “test” and “definition.”[[/CHANGED]]  
  [[NEW]]What this question is really asking: “How is the author checking whether his definition works?”[[/NEW]]

- **Original question B:** In your own words, explain at least three reasons why Lowe thinks his definition that community is “a shared story” works.
- **Modified question B for Jasmine:** [[CHANGED]]Explain 3 reasons Lowe says community is a “shared story.” You may use the bullets and write short phrases.[[/CHANGED]]  
  [[NEW]]What this question is really asking: “Why does the author think his definition makes sense?”[[/NEW]]

### 6) Whole Class Reading — paragraphs 9–11 — 5 min
- **Original step:** Students read and answer the During Reading Questions.
- **Modified step for Jasmine:** [[CHANGED]]Before reading paragraph 11, point Jasmine to the last two paragraphs and ask her to highlight the sentence that best answers the lesson title. Then have her restate it orally in 10 words or less before she writes.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[NEW]]Goal 3 — ELA benchmark: “Write a claim that accurately answers each part of the question.”[[/NEW]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Two highlighters: one for “community is…” and one for “why it matters.”[[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[NEW]]At the transition into the final text section, before the “Why does this matter?” heading.[[/NEW]]

#### During Reading Questions — Paragraphs 10–11
- **Original question A:** Highlight two pieces of evidence that answer both questions.
- **Modified question A for Jasmine:** [[CHANGED]]Highlight 2 sentences that answer the title question: “What is community?” and “Why is it important?”[[/CHANGED]]  
  [[NEW]]What this question is really asking: “Which two lines best prove the main point?”[[/NEW]]

### 7) Independent Practice MCQ — 5 min
- **Original step:** Students complete multiple choice questions.
- **Modified step for Jasmine:** [[CHANGED]]Give Jasmine only the same four multiple-choice items on a clean page with a line under each question where she writes one key word from the text before choosing an answer. Keep her in the room; do not shorten the standard.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[NEW]]Goal 3 — ELA: “Independently answer literal and inferential comprehension questions”; accommodation “Reminder to remain engaged.”[[/NEW]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Clean MCQ page with “key word first” line; text box at top: “Find the sentence in the article that helps.”[[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[NEW]]The minute Independent Practice starts, before she sees Question 1.[[/NEW]]

#### MCQ support note
- [[NEW]]If she hesitates on Question 2 or 4, point her back to the paragraph number in the question, not to the answer choices.[[/NEW]]  
  [[NEW]]Why: this keeps the task aligned to RI.7.2 and supports her need to reread and track text without lowering the reading demand.[[/NEW]]

### 8) Independent Practice Short Response — 15 min
- **Original step:** Students answer the short response prompt.
- **Modified step for Jasmine:** [[CHANGED]]Keep the same prompt, but give Jasmine a sentence-stem box and a 3-step checklist tied to the rubric. Ask her to draft two sentences first, then add one piece of evidence from the article, then check that she explained how the evidence supports the answer.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[NEW]]Goal 3 — ELA benchmark: “Write a claim that accurately answers each part of the question,” “Independently find effective pieces of textual evidence to support the claim,” and “Write analysis showing how evidence supports the claim.”[[/NEW]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Response frame, evidence bank, and checklist on one page.[[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[NEW]]As soon as the MCQs are finished, before she begins the short response.[[/NEW]]

### 9) Student-Led Discussion — 5 min
- **Original step:** Students discuss the questions with a partner and record both answers.
- **Modified step for Jasmine:** [[CHANGED]]Keep Jasmine with a partner in the same classroom. Give her one discussion card with the three questions shortened to one line each and a box for “My answer” plus “Partner’s answer.” Ask her to start with question 1 only, then move to questions 2 and 3 if she is still engaged.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[NEW]]Strength: “Likes participating, open to feedback during 1-on-1 check-ins, warm, gets along with peers”; accommodation “1:1 check-ins”; placement “Full Inclusion Program (services outside gen ed < 21% of time; 80% inclusion).”[[/NEW]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Discussion card with sentence stems and a check box for “I spoke,” “I listened,” and “I added on.”[[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[NEW]]During the final 5 minutes, after written work is complete.[[/NEW]]
