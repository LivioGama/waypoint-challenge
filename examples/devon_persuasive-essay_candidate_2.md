# Tomorrow's Lesson — Devon (Grade 7)

## PART A — Teacher Cheat Sheet

### Before the bell
- [[NEW]]Put Devon’s modified question sheet on his desk before class starts, with the prompt page already open to **Independent Practice** and the answer choices already spaced out. This takes under a minute and keeps him from facing a blank page. [[/NEW]]
- [[NEW]]Place a copy of Devon’s warning-signs sheet and a red card on the corner of his desk where he can reach them without asking. [[/NEW]]
- [[NEW]]Write one short sentence on a sticky note: “You can start with the thesis box first, then move to reasons.” Hand it to him when he enters. [[/NEW]]

### Watch for
- [[NEW]]He goes quiet or freezes right after the class turns to **Partner Reading** or **Turn & Talk**. That fits his profile because he prefers writing over speaking and whole-group speaking often turns into blank work. [[/NEW]]
- [[NEW]]He stares at the blank lines in **Independent Practice** or only writes one sentence. That matches his present level: strong writing in a quiet setting, but in whole-group he often turns in blank paper or a single sentence. [[/NEW]]
- [[NEW]]He starts erasing hard, pushing the paper away, or asking to leave after a timed direction or a fast discussion question. That fits his anxiety triggers: timed tasks, cold-calling, and unexpected performance pressure. [[/NEW]]

### If they disengage
Use this exact 2-step script, with the coping move already named in the IEP:
1. [[CHANGED]]“I see the warning sign. Take your red-card break now.”[[/CHANGED]]
2. [[CHANGED]]“Do 60 seconds of box breathing, then come back and start with the thesis box.”[[/CHANGED]]

This leans on **Goal 1 — Anxiety self-management**: “deploy a taught coping strategy (box breathing, 5-4-3-2-1 grounding, red-card break, request co-regulation)” and the benchmark “Complete a 60-second box-breathing cycle before re-entering classroom, 4/5 opps.”

### Praise specifically
- “[[NEW]]You named your position clearly and started with the thesis box right away — that’s strong persuasive writing. [[/NEW]]”
- “[[NEW]]You used evidence that matches your claim, and you kept the task moving without shutting down. [[/NEW]]”
- “[[NEW]]You stayed in the room, finished one chunk, and returned to the next box — that’s the kind of work stamina we’re building. [[/NEW]]”

## PART B — Modified Lesson Plan

### 1) Whole Class Reading
- **Original step:** “Today we're going to start by looking at what makes an argument persuasive. Turn to your neighbor and talk about a time when someone convinced you to change your mind about something. What did they say? How did they convince you?”
- **Modified step for Devon:** [[CHANGED]]Give Devon the same opening idea, but let him respond by writing one example on a sticky note instead of turning and talking. After he writes, ask him to underline the part that changed someone’s mind. [[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** **Goal 2 — Written expression — at grade level**: “Strong written work when given choice of topic and quiet setting.” **Goal 1 — Anxiety self-management**: “deploy a taught coping strategy… before escalation.”  
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Sticky note response frame: “Someone changed my mind when ____. They convinced me by ____.” [[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[CHANGED]]At the start of the lesson, during the first 5 minutes, before any partner talk. [[/CHANGED]]

### 2) Model Essay Look-Fors
- **Original step:** “Now, let's look at a model persuasive essay. Pay attention to: the clear statement of what the writer believes; the reasons and evidence they provide; how they handle disagreement.”
- **Modified step for Devon:** [[SAME]]Keep this whole-class model reading, but give Devon a 3-box note strip with the three look-fors already printed. He checks each box while listening/reading. [[/SAME]]
- **IEP citation:** **Goal 2 — Written expression — at grade level**: “Written response accepted in place of any oral response.”  
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]3-box note strip: “Thesis / Reasons + evidence / Counterargument.” [[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[SAME]]During the model essay read-aloud or silent read, minutes 5–20. [[/SAME]]

### 3) Partner Reading Questions
- **Original step:** “Work with a partner to analyze the essay. Answer these questions together: 1. What is the writer trying to convince us of? 2. What evidence do they use? 3. Is their argument convincing? Why or why not?”
- **Modified step for Devon:** [[CHANGED]]Let Devon answer the three analysis questions in writing first, then share one response only if he raises his hand or signals. Do not ask him to speak first. [[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** **Accommodation — Presentation of instruction**: “No cold-calling — Devon raises hand or signals to participate” and “Written response accepted in place of any oral response.” **Goal 1 — Anxiety self-management**: “before escalation.”  
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Mini-answer space with sentence starters: “The writer is trying to convince us that ____.” “One piece of evidence is ____.” “This is / is not convincing because ____.” [[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[CHANGED]]During Partner Reading, minutes 20–30. [[/CHANGED]]

### 4) During Reading Questions A–D
- **Original step:**  
  A. Think & Share — “After reading the introduction, what do you think this essay will argue?”  
  B. Turn & Talk — “Do you agree or disagree with the writer's main point so far?”  
  C. Write — “Jot down one piece of evidence the writer uses and whether it supports their claim.”  
  D. Find Evidence — “Highlight one sentence where the writer acknowledges a different point of view.”
- **Modified step for Devon:** [[CHANGED]]Keep A and C as writing responses only, skip the spoken Turn & Talk for Devon, and have him highlight D in the text with a sticky tab instead of just finding it mentally. [[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** **Accommodation — Presentation of instruction**: “Written directions given in writing whenever oral directions are given.” **Accommodation — Response**: “Written response accepted in place of any oral response.” **Goal 2 — Written expression — at grade level**: “strong written work when given choice of topic and quiet setting.”  
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]One-page response strip with three boxes: “Prediction / Evidence / Counterpoint sentence.” [[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[CHANGED]]Immediately after reading the model essay, minutes 20–30. [[/CHANGED]]

### 5) Independent Practice
- **Original step:** “Now it's your turn. Choose a social issue you care about—it could be about your school, your community, or something global. Write down: 1. The issue 2. Your position 3. Three reasons why you believe this 4. Evidence for each reason.”
- **Modified step for Devon:** [[CHANGED]]Give Devon a preselected issue menu with 3 choices, then have him complete the planning sheet in this order: issue → position → three reasons → evidence. If he stalls, point him back to the next blank box, not the whole page. [[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** **Goal 2 — Written expression — at grade level**: “Use a written planning template (claim → 3 evidence → conclusion) before drafting, 4/5 assignments.” and “Complete first paragraph in gen ed setting before optional move to alternate setting, 3/5 assignments.”  
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Planning sheet with boxes already labeled: “Issue / Claim / Reason 1 / Evidence 1 / Reason 2 / Evidence 2 / Reason 3 / Evidence 3 / Closing idea.” [[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[CHANGED]]During Independent Practice, minutes 30–40. [[/CHANGED]]

### 6) Student-Led Discussion
- **Original step:** “In small groups, share your chosen issues. Help each other by asking: Is your position clear? Are your reasons strong? What evidence could support this better?”
- **Modified step for Devon:** [[CHANGED]]Let Devon stay in the small group, but give him a written feedback card so he can point to the question he wants to ask or answer instead of speaking first. [[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** **Accommodation — Response**: “Written response accepted in place of any oral response.” **Placement:** inclusion; do not pull him out when he prefers whole group.  
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Written feedback card: “My position is clear because ____.” “My strongest reason is ____.” “I still need evidence for ____.” [[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[CHANGED]]During the final 5 minutes, after Independent Practice. [[/CHANGED]]

### 7) Exit/Check for Understanding
- **Original step:** No separate exit ticket listed.
- **Modified step for Devon:** [[NEW]]Collect a 1-minute written check before dismissal: “What is your claim?” and “What evidence do you have for it?” [[/NEW]]
- **IEP citation:** **Goal 2 — Written expression — at grade level**: “Submit final draft (typed) within agreed extended deadline, 4/5 assignments.”  
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Two-line exit slip with sentence starters: “My claim is ____.” “One piece of evidence is ____.” [[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[NEW]]At the end of class, after small-group discussion and before students leave. [[/NEW]]

**Answer: B**

[[NEW]]What this question is really asking: [[NEW]] “Which choice sounds most trustworthy and backed by facts?” [[/NEW]]
