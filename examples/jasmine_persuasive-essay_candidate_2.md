# Tomorrow's Lesson — Jasmine (Grade 7)

## PART A — Teacher Cheat Sheet

### Before the bell
- [[NEW]]Put Jasmine’s modified question sheet on her desk before students enter, with the model-essay questions already boxed in and a sticky note on the page that says, “Pause, plan, proceed.”[[/NEW]]
- [[NEW]]Pre-mark one spot in the model essay where she will highlight evidence and one spot where she will underline the counterargument sentence.[[/NEW]]
- [[NEW]]Set out a short reference strip with the 3 key words she’ll need today: thesis, evidence, counterargument. Keep it on the desk with the graphic organizer.[[/NEW]]

### Watch for
- [[NEW]]Head down, because this lesson asks her to hold onto a model essay, answer partner questions, and then switch to her own topic; that’s the exact pattern that can trigger frustration for her when the work feels long.[[/NEW]]
- [[NEW]]Repeated restroom requests after the independent topic choice, since her profile says frustration often turns into avoidance.[[/NEW]]
- [[NEW]]Stopping after she names a social issue but before she fills in reasons and evidence; she tends to need help breaking big writing tasks into smaller parts.[[/NEW]]

### If they disengage
Use the IEP self-regulation goal strategy already named:
1. [[NEW]]Quietly point to the desk strip and say, “Take two deep breaths with me, then choose a grounding move or a quick movement break.”[[/NEW]]
2. [[NEW]]After the break, say, “Now come back to the organizer and answer just the next box.”[[/NEW]]

### Praise specifically
- [[NEW]]“You just wrote a claim that answers the question clearly — that matches the thesis work we need today.”[[/NEW]]
- [[NEW]]“You found evidence that fits your point, not just any fact. That’s strong persuasive writing.”[[/NEW]]
- [[NEW]]“You stayed with the task after the hard part and came back to it — that’s exactly what strong writers do.”[[/NEW]]

## PART B — Modified Lesson Plan

### 1) Lesson opening / activate prior knowledge
- **Original step:** “Turn to your neighbor and talk about a time when someone convinced you to change your mind about something.”
- [[CHANGED]]Jasmine still turns and talks with the class partner, but I give her a 2-question oral choice card: “What changed your mind?” and “What did the person say?” She can answer with words or short phrases.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** Goal 3 — ELA: “Independently answer literal and inferential comprehension questions or writing prompts that meet grade-level rubrics, from 50% → 75%.” Present Levels: “Struggles with literal/inferential questions verbally and in writing.”
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** Partner-talk card with two sentence starters: “I changed my mind when…” / “They convinced me by…”
- **When:** During the whole-class reading opener, before the model essay is introduced.

### 2) Mini-lesson: define persuasive writing
- **Original step:** Teacher explains persuasive writing and reads the lesson definition.
- [[SAME]]Keep the class definition and read-aloud, but let Jasmine keep the 3-word reference strip on her desk and track each word with her finger as you say it.[[/SAME]]
- **IEP citation:** Goal 3 — ELA benchmark: “Accurately annotate a given text for the prompt — labeled annotation focus, supporting detail, etc.” Accommodation: “Copy of teacher's notes.”
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** 3-word reference strip: thesis / evidence / counterargument.
- **When:** Right after the persuasive-writing definition in lines 134–147.

### 3) Read the model essay together
- **Original step:** “Read the model essay aloud or have students read it silently, then discuss as a class.”
- [[CHANGED]]Read the model essay aloud to the whole class while Jasmine gets a copy with three highlights already set: thesis in one color, evidence in another, counterargument in a third. She marks the same three spots as you read.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** Accommodation: “Copy of teacher's notes”; Goal 3 benchmark: “Accurately annotate a given text for the prompt — labeled annotation focus, supporting detail, etc.”
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** Color-coded annotation copy of the model essay.
- **When:** During the model-essay reading portion.

### 4) Analyze the essay structure
- **Original step:** “Pay attention to: the clear statement of what the writer believes; the reasons and evidence they provide; how they handle disagreement.”
- [[CHANGED]]Instead of asking Jasmine to hold all three ideas in her head, I hand her a 3-box organizer titled “What is the claim?” “What evidence supports it?” “How does the writer handle disagreement?” She fills one box at a time with the class.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** Goal 3 benchmark: “Write a claim that accurately answers each part of the question.” Present Levels: “Needs significant adult support to break down vocabulary, sentence structures, key ideas, and to organize thinking.”
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** 3-box organizer aligned to the model essay.
- **When:** During the whole-class model analysis, before partner reading.

### 5) Partner reading questions
- **Original step:** “Work with a partner to analyze the essay. Answer these questions together: 1. What is the writer trying to convince us of? 2. What evidence do they use? 3. Is their argument convincing? Why or why not?”
- [[CHANGED]]Jasmine answers the same 3 questions, but I add a one-line “think first” box under each: “The writer wants us to believe…” / “One piece of evidence is…” / “I think it is convincing because…”[/CHANGED]
- **IEP citation:** Goal 3 benchmark: “Independently answer literal comprehension questions (main idea, setting, plot)” and “Independently find effective pieces of textual evidence to support the claim.”
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** Modified partner-response sheet with sentence stems.
- **When:** During partner reading, after the class reads the model essay.

### 6) During-reading question A
- **Original step:** “After reading the introduction, what do you think this essay will argue?”
- [[CHANGED]]Jasmine gets this question in simpler language next to the original: “What do you think the writer will try to prove?” She answers in one short sentence before sharing.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** Goal 3 benchmark: “Write a claim that accurately answers each part of the question.”
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** Side-by-side question rewrite with a single sentence stem.
- **When:** Immediately after the introduction is read.

### 7) During-reading question B
- **Original step:** “With your neighbor, discuss: Do you agree or disagree with the writer's main point so far?”
- [[SAME]]Keep the class discussion, but Jasmine can answer by pointing to one of two response cards: “I agree because…” or “I disagree because…”[[/SAME]]
- **IEP citation:** Goal 3 baseline: “Difficulty accessing grade-level texts; often does not annotate.” Strength: “Enjoys peer talk.”
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** Two response cards.
- **When:** During the turn-and-talk after the introduction.

### 8) During-reading question C
- **Original step:** “Jot down one piece of evidence the writer uses and whether it supports their claim.”
- [[CHANGED]]Jasmine gets a two-column note box: “Evidence” / “Supports the claim? Yes or no.” She only has to copy one piece of evidence and circle yes or no.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** Goal 3 benchmark: “Independently find effective pieces of textual evidence to support the claim.”
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** Two-column evidence note box.
- **When:** During the write/jot moment.

### 9) During-reading question D
- **Original step:** “Highlight one sentence where the writer acknowledges a different point of view.”
- [[SAME]]Jasmine highlights the sentence, and I ask her to add a quick margin label: “counterargument.”[[/SAME]]
- **IEP citation:** Goal 3 benchmark: “Accurately annotate a given text for the prompt — labeled annotation focus, supporting detail, etc.”
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** Margin label on the same model text.
- **When:** During the evidence-finding step.

### 10) Independent practice: choose a social issue
- **Original step:** “Choose a social issue you care about… Write down: 1. The issue 2. Your position 3. Three reasons why you believe this 4. Evidence for each reason.”
- [[CHANGED]]Jasmine uses a topic menu with 4 teacher-approved social issues from the lesson examples, then narrows to one by circling a topic she already knows something about. She completes the organizer one box at a time with a quick check-in after box 2.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** Present Levels: “Benefits immensely from small group / 1:1”; “Needs significant adult support to break down vocabulary, sentence structures, key ideas, and to organize thinking.” Placement: inclusion/whole group preference.
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** Topic menu + one-box-at-a-time organizer + check-in after the position box.
- **When:** At the start of independent practice.

### 11) Independent practice: reasons and evidence
- **Original step:** “Three reasons why you believe this; Evidence for each reason.”
- [[CHANGED]]Instead of expecting three finished reasons on the first pass, Jasmine drafts reason 1 with evidence, then gets a 20-second teacher check before moving to reason 2 and 3. If she stalls, she uses the class notes and the reference strip to choose evidence language.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** Goal 3 benchmark: “Independently find effective pieces of textual evidence to support the claim.” Accommodation: “Reference sheets, graphic organizers, and checklists.”
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** Checkpoint routine + reference strip + organizer.
- **When:** During independent practice after topic selection.

### 12) Student-led discussion
- **Original step:** “In small groups, share your chosen issues…”
- [[SAME]]Keep her in the whole-group / classroom discussion format she prefers; she shares only after she has written the issue and position, and she may read directly from her organizer.[[/SAME]]
- **IEP citation:** Student profile: “chooses whole group 80% of the time when small group offered.” Placement: inclusion.
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** She reads from the organizer instead of creating a new response on the spot.
- **When:** During the final share-out.

## PART C — Modified Student Materials

### A. Partner Reading Questions — Jasmine version
**Directions:** Answer the same questions. You may use short phrases.

1. [[CHANGED]]What is the writer trying to convince us of?[[/CHANGED]]  
   [[NEW]]What this question is really asking: What is the author’s main point or claim?[[/NEW]]  
   **Answer frame:** The writer is trying to convince us that __________________.

2. [[CHANGED]]What evidence do they use?[[/CHANGED]]  
   [[NEW]]What this question is really asking: What fact, example, or quote helps prove the claim?[[/NEW]]  
   **Answer frame:** One piece of evidence is __________________.

3. [[CHANGED]]Is their argument convincing? Why or why not?[[/CHANGED]]  
   [[NEW]]What this question is really asking: Do the reasons make sense and feel strong?[[/NEW]]  
   **Answer frame:** It is convincing because __________________. / It is not convincing because __________________.

### B. During-Reading Questions — Jasmine version
1. [[CHANGED]]After reading the introduction, what do you think this essay will argue?[[/CHANGED]]  
   [[NEW]]What this question is really asking: What will the writer try to prove?[[/NEW]]  
   **Answer frame:** I think the essay will argue that __________________.

2. [[CHANGED]]Do you agree or disagree with the writer's main point so far?[[/CHANGED]]  
   [[NEW]]What this question is really asking: Is the writer’s point one you could support?[[/NEW]]  
   **Answer frame:** I agree / disagree because __________________.

3. [[CHANGED]]Jot down one piece of evidence the writer uses and whether it supports their claim.[[/CHANGED]]  
   [[NEW]]What this question is really asking: Copy one proof and tell if it helps the argument.[[/NEW]]  
   **Answer box:**  
   - Evidence: __________________  
   - Supports the claim? Yes / No

4. [[CHANGED]]Highlight one sentence where the writer acknowledges a different point of view.[[/CHANGED]]  
   [[NEW]]What this question is really asking: Find the sentence where the writer shows the other side.[[/NEW]]  
   **Answer box:** Sentence: __________________
