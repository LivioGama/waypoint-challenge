### Before the bell
- [[NEW]]Open Marcus’s TTS file for the article and test that headphones work at his seat near the outlet.[[/NEW]]  
  [[NEW]]Why: Goal 3 — Self-advocacy says he should “independently request and set up his accommodations (TTS, headphones, extra time)” and the accommodation says “Text-to-speech for any text > 1 paragraph.”[[/NEW]]

### Watch for
- He stares at the first dense paragraph and stops tracking after the sentence with multiple commas and clauses.  
  [[NEW]]This fits his present level: “Without TTS, his comprehension collapses around dense or multi-clause sentences.”[[/NEW]]
- He jokes off the task or says he already knows it, then avoids opening the text.  
  [[NEW]]This is a common cover for reading stress, and the lesson’s first text block is print-heavy.[[/NEW]]
- He looks at the independent practice and freezes on the wording of #2 or the short response prompt.  
  [[NEW]]His present level says print-only work drops to “30%–60%” and writing mechanics are “well below grade level.”[[/NEW]]

### If they disengage
1. [[CHANGED]]Say: “Marcus, let’s switch to headphones and the audio now. Start with Paragraph 1 while I point to the line.”[[/CHANGED]]  
   [[NEW]]This uses the IEP accommodation “Text-to-speech for any text > 1 paragraph” and keeps him in whole group, which fits his placement in “Full Inclusion Program.”[[/NEW]]
2. [[CHANGED]]Then say: “After this paragraph, tell me one sentence in your own words or say it to me and I’ll type it.”[[/CHANGED]]  
   [[NEW]]This uses “Oral responses accepted in place of writing for comprehension checks” and supports Goal 2 and Goal 3 without pulling him out.[[/NEW]]

### Praise specifically
- “[[NEW]]You caught the central idea by listening for the big claim and the details that prove it.[[/NEW]]”
- “[[NEW]]You used the audio to stay with the text even when the sentence got packed with ideas — that’s strong reader behavior.[[/NEW]]”
- “[[NEW]]You named an accommodation yourself and kept working with the class. That’s exactly what independent self-advocacy looks like.[[/NEW]]”

## PART B — Modified Lesson Plan

### 1) Intro Slide Deck — 5 min
- **Original step:** Guide students through the slide deck to introduce them to the reading lesson and skill focus.
- **Modified step for Marcus:** [[CHANGED]]Keep Marcus with the whole class for the slide deck, but give him the 1-sentence purpose statement on paper and read it aloud once while he follows along on the slide.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[NEW]]Goal 1 — Reading fluency: “Track text without finger or marker for 1 minute.” Present level: “Listening comprehension at G7.”[[/NEW]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Purpose strip with one sentence: “We are finding the central idea and the details that support it.” Add a box that says “Central idea = what the text is mostly about.”[[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[NEW]]At the start of the lesson, before the class begins the article.[[/NEW]]

### 2) Vocabulary preview — embedded in intro
- **Original step:** Let’s pronounce these words together as a class: aspect, moral, narrative, specific.
- **Modified step for Marcus:** [[CHANGED]]Have Marcus hear each word, say it once with the class, and point to a tiny margin definition card instead of copying definitions.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[NEW]]Accommodation: “Vocabulary previewed orally before reading.” Present level: “With text-to-speech, Marcus performs grade-appropriate analytical work.”[[/NEW]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Four mini cards with plain meanings: aspect = part; moral = about right/wrong; narrative = story; specific = exact.[[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[NEW]]During the vocabulary moment on the intro slide deck.[[/NEW]]

### 3) Whole Class Reading — Paragraphs 1–2 — 15 min
- **Original step:** Students read and answer the During Reading Questions.
- **Modified step for Marcus:** [[CHANGED]]Marcus listens with headphones while the teacher or audio reads Paragraphs 1–2; he tracks the text with a finger only if he wants to, then answers Question A and B by either speaking to the teacher or marking the answer on his sheet.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[NEW]]Accommodation: “Text-to-speech for any text > 1 paragraph,” “Oral responses accepted in place of writing for comprehension checks,” and “Headphones permitted for TTS without singling out.” Present level: “Without TTS, his comprehension collapses around dense or multi-clause sentences.”[[/NEW]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]For A and B, add two answer frames:
  - A: “Lowe says community is ______.”
  - B: “The bulleted list shows ______ because ______.”
  Also add a one-box note: “Listen for the claim and the list.”[[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[NEW]]Right after Paragraph 2 is read, before the class shares out.[[/NEW]]

### 4) During Reading Question C — Paragraph 2
- **Original step:** Turn & Talk: Based on the bulleted list, summarize at least three key traits of a community.
- **Modified step for Marcus:** [[CHANGED]]Let Marcus give three traits orally to a partner while looking at the bullet list; the partner can jot them down if needed, but Marcus does not need to copy full sentences.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[NEW]]Goal 2 — Written expression with assistive tech: “dictate two pieces of supporting evidence with paragraph references.” Present level: “ideas are strong; spelling and mechanics are well below grade level.”[[/NEW]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Three boxes labeled “trait 1 / trait 2 / trait 3” with word bank options: different kinds, belonging, in-group/out-group, moral ideas, multiple communities.[[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[NEW]]Immediately after the teacher finishes Paragraph 2 and before moving to Paragraphs 3–7.[[/NEW]]

### 5) Partner Reading — Paragraphs 3–7 — 10 min
- **Original step:** Students read the next section and answer the During Reading Questions.
- **Modified step for Marcus:** [[CHANGED]]Marcus stays in the partner-reading setup, but he uses TTS for Paragraphs 3–7 and answers the Turn & Talk question by speaking first, then optionally typing one sentence with speech-to-text.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[NEW]]Accommodation: “Text-to-speech for any text > 1 paragraph,” “Speech-to-text for written responses > 2 sentences,” and “Never require unprompted oral reading.” Present level: “With text-to-speech, comprehension ~grade level on the same text.”[[/NEW]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Add a sentence stem card: “Lowe’s community fits because ______. It shows this when he says ______.”[[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[NEW]]During the partner-reading block, after Paragraph 7 is reached.[[/NEW]]

### 6) Whole Class Reading — Paragraph 8 — 5 min
- **Original step:** Students read Paragraph 8 and answer the question about why the author includes another bulleted list.
- **Modified step for Marcus:** [[CHANGED]]Pause after the first two bullets in Paragraph 8 and ask Marcus to point to which earlier paragraph the author is comparing; he can answer orally instead of writing.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[NEW]]Goal 1 — Reading fluency benchmark: “Re-read for fluency after first cold read.” Present level: “Reading rate ~75 wpm (G7 target ~150).”[[/NEW]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]A side note on his page: “Compare Paragraph 8 to Paragraph 2.” and a two-choice oral prompt: “Is the author testing the definition or telling a new story?”[[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[NEW]]Right after Paragraph 8 begins, before the class answers.[[/NEW]]

### 7) Independent Practice — Multiple Choice #1–4 — 5 min
- **Original step:** Students answer the multiple choice questions.
- **Modified step for Marcus:** [[CHANGED]]Give Marcus the same four questions in a clean version with fewer distracting words, then have him answer by circling choices on paper or verbally stating answers while the teacher marks them.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[NEW]]Accommodation: “Oral responses accepted in place of writing for comprehension checks” and “Spelling errors not penalized.” Present level: “Strong oral participation; struggles with dense print.”[[/NEW]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]A reduced-load question sheet with one-sentence glosses under each item:
  - #1 gloss: “What is the article mostly trying to do?”
  - #2 gloss: “Which quote best proves the main idea?”
  - #3 gloss: “What does slighted mean here?”
  - #4 gloss: “How do paragraphs 5–7 help explain the main idea?”[[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[NEW]]At the start of Independent Practice, before he begins #1.[[/NEW]]

### 8) Independent Practice — Short Response — 15 min
- **Original step:** Students answer the short response prompt.
- **Modified step for Marcus:** [[CHANGED]]Let Marcus dictate his response into speech-to-text or to an adult scribe, using a 5-sentence frame that still requires the same claim, evidence, and explanation.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[NEW]]Goal 2 — Written expression with assistive tech: “Produce a 5-sentence on-grade analytical paragraph using speech-to-text + spell check, with 4 of 5 sentences free of meaning-altering errors.” Benchmarks: “Dictate a complete claim sentence answering the prompt” and “Dictate two pieces of supporting evidence with paragraph references.”[[/NEW]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Add this frame:
  1. “Lowe means community is ______.”
  2. “This is shown when the text says ______.”
  3. “Another detail is ______.”
  4. “This means ______.”
  5. “So, a community is ______.”
  Include a checklist: claim, 2 details, unit vocabulary, punctuation check.[[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[NEW]]The moment the short response begins, after #4 is finished.[[/NEW]]

### 9) Student-Led Discussion — 5 min
- **Original step:** Students discuss the questions with a partner.
- **Modified step for Marcus:** [[CHANGED]]Keep Marcus in the partner discussion and give him one choice of which question to answer first; he may answer orally and his partner can record it.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[NEW]]Goal 3 — Self-advocacy: “Politely decline unprompted oral reading without disengaging.” Present level: “Responds well to choice (which scaffold to use).”[[/NEW]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Add a tiny choice box: “Answer Q1 / Q2 / Q3 first” and a starter: “In my community, courteous behavior is ______.”[[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[NEW]]At the start of the 5-minute discussion block.[[/NEW]]
