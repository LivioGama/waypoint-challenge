# Tomorrow's Lesson — Marcus (Grade 7)

## Part A — Teacher Cheat Sheet

### Before the bell
- [[NEW]]Put Marcus’s headphones and a charged device at his seat before students enter. Open the text in a TTS-ready format so he can press play right away.[[/NEW]]  
  - IEP: Goal 3 — Self-advocacy, benchmark: “Set up TTS on a new device unprompted.”  
  - Accommodation: “Text-to-speech for any text > 1 paragraph” and “Headphones permitted for TTS without singling out”
- [[NEW]]Print the modified question sheet and the graphic organizer, then place them face-up at his desk.[[/NEW]]  
  - IEP: Goal 2 — Written expression with assistive tech, benchmark: “Dictate a complete claim sentence answering the prompt.”
- [[NEW]]Pre-highlight the lesson’s key words on a sticky note: community, identity-forming narrative, central idea, detail, belonging, narrative.[[/NEW]]  
  - IEP: Accommodation: “Vocabulary previewed orally before reading”

### Watch for
- [[NEW]]He starts rereading the same dense sentence silently, loses his place, or looks up blankly at the paragraph with multiple clauses.[[/NEW]]  
  - IEP link: “With text-to-speech, Marcus performs grade-appropriate analytical work; without it, his comprehension collapses around dense or multi-clause sentences.”
- [[NEW]]He jokes, shrugs, or goes quiet when asked to read or answer from the page without audio.[[/NEW]]  
  - IEP link: “Avoidance of any task that requires reading aloud unprompted” and “Never require unprompted oral reading.”
- [[NEW]]He answers a partner question well out loud but leaves the written space empty, especially during Independent Practice or Discussion.[[/NEW]]  
  - IEP link: Goal 2 — “Produce a 5-sentence on-grade analytical paragraph using speech-to-text + spell check”

### If they disengage
1. [[CHANGED]]Say: “Marcus, let’s switch to audio for this part. Put on headphones and press play on paragraph 2.”[[/CHANGED]]  
2. [[CHANGED]]Then say: “Tell me your answer out loud first, and I’ll help you turn it into one sentence on the organizer.”[[/CHANGED]]  
   - IEP support used: “Text-to-speech for any text > 1 paragraph” and “Oral responses accepted in place of writing for comprehension checks”

### Praise specifically
- [[NEW]]“You just explained the central idea clearly from the audio — that’s strong grade 7 thinking.”[[/NEW]]
- [[NEW]]“I liked how you used the text to back up your answer without getting stuck on the reading.”[[/NEW]]
- [[NEW]]“You set up your headphones and got started fast — that’s exactly how a self-advocate uses accommodations.”[[/NEW]]

## Part B — Modified Lesson Plan

### 1) Intro Slide Deck (5 min)

- **Original step:** Guide students through the slide deck to introduce them to the reading lesson and skill focus.
- **Modified step for Marcus:** [[CHANGED]]Have Marcus follow the slides with the class, but while the teacher introduces the skill, hand him the 6-word oral preview card and let him repeat the key vocabulary once with the class: community, narrative, central idea, detail, belonging, evidence.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** “Vocabulary previewed orally before reading”; Present level: “Listening comprehension at G7.”
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]6-word oral preview card + 1-sentence student-friendly meaning beside each word.[[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[CHANGED]]During the first 2 minutes of the Intro Slide Deck, before the class opens the text.

---

### 2) Purpose for Reading / Vocabulary

- **Original step:** Students read the purpose and pronounce vocabulary together.
- **Modified step for Marcus:** [[CHANGED]]Read the purpose aloud to the class and hand Marcus the same text with the key sentence highlighted; ask him to restate it in his own words after listening.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** Present level: “Listening comprehension is at or above grade level”; Accommodation: “Text-to-speech for any text > 1 paragraph.”
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Highlighted purpose strip and 2-word margin notes: “definition” / “why important.”[[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[CHANGED]]Right after the teacher says the purpose for reading, before students begin paragraph 1.

---

### 3) Whole Class Reading — Paragraphs 1–2

- **Original step:** Whole class reads paragraphs 1–2 and answers questions A–C.
- **Modified step for Marcus:** [[CHANGED]]Have Marcus listen to paragraphs 1–2 with TTS on headphones while following the text, then answer Question A orally to you or a partner before the class shares out.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** “Text-to-speech for any text > 1 paragraph”; Goal 3 benchmark: “Politely decline unprompted oral reading without disengaging.”
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Question card with a short answer frame: “Lowe says community is powerful because ____.”[[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[CHANGED]]During the first pause after paragraph 1 and again after paragraph 2.

#### During Reading Question B
- **Original step:** Write: What is the relationship between the claim above and the bulleted list in paragraph 2?
- **Modified step for Marcus:** [[CHANGED]]Let Marcus answer using the organizer: first say the claim, then point to one bullet that supports it, then say “the list shows that a good definition must ____.”[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** Goal 2 benchmark: “Dictate two pieces of supporting evidence with paragraph references.”
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Sentence stem: “The claim and the list connect because the list explains ____.”[[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[CHANGED]]Immediately after paragraph 2 is read, before partner talk.

#### During Reading Question C
- **Original step:** Turn & Talk: Based on the bulleted list, summarize at least three key traits of a community.
- **Modified step for Marcus:** [[CHANGED]]Keep the partner talk, but require Marcus to give three spoken traits first and then check off the same three on his organizer.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** Present level: “Strong oral participation”; Accommodation: “Oral responses accepted in place of writing for comprehension checks.”
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]3-box organizer labeled: types, feelings, shared story.[[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[CHANGED]]At the end of the first reading segment, during partner talk.

---

### 4) Partner Reading — Paragraphs 3–7

- **Original step:** Partner reading and questions A–B.
- **Modified step for Marcus:** [[CHANGED]]Keep Marcus in the partner pair, but make him the listener first. He follows along with TTS, then his partner reads one short chunk aloud while Marcus uses the audio to track the same chunk. He answers Question A orally after paragraph 7.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** “Never require unprompted oral reading”; Present level: “Without TTS, his comprehension collapses around dense or multi-clause sentences.”
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Chunk labels added to the text: Paragraph 3, 4, 5, 6, 7.[[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[CHANGED]]Throughout the partner-reading block.

#### During Reading Question A
- **Original step:** How does Lowe’s example of belonging to the Newcastle community support his definition?
- **Modified step for Marcus:** [[CHANGED]]Before the partner discussion, have Marcus say: “The Newcastle example supports the definition because ____.” Then he points to one sentence from paragraph 5 or 7.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** Goal 2 benchmark: “Dictate a complete claim sentence answering the prompt.”
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Sentence stem + evidence box with paragraph numbers 5 and 7.[[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[CHANGED]]Right after paragraph 7 is finished.

#### During Reading Question B
- **Original step:** Turn & Talk: Consider the communities you are a part of...
- **Modified step for Marcus:** [[CHANGED]]Let Marcus choose one community he knows well and answer orally in two parts: what the community does and how the shared story shows up in behavior.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** Present level: “Asks sharp clarifying questions”; Goal 3 benchmark: “Name his accommodations when asked” is linked by asking him to choose his response mode.
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Two-part response frame: “In my community, people usually ____ . That shows the shared story because ____.”[[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[CHANGED]]At the end of paragraph 7, before the class moves to paragraph 8.

---

### 5) Whole Class Reading — Paragraph 8

- **Original step:** Whole class reads paragraph 8 and answers Question A/B.
- **Modified step for Marcus:** [[CHANGED]]Have Marcus listen to paragraph 8 with TTS, then sort the six criteria into “matches the definition” or “explains the definition” using a two-column organizer instead of copying the list.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** Present level: “Strong with diagrams and physical models”; Accommodation: “Spelling errors not penalized.”
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Two-column sort card with the six criteria already typed in short phrases.[[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[CHANGED]]During the reading pause after paragraph 8, before the class discussion.

#### During Reading Question A
- **Original step:** Why does the author include another bulleted list in this section?
- **Modified step for Marcus:** [[CHANGED]]Ask Marcus to answer orally with this frame: “The second list is there to show that Lowe is testing his idea against the six traits.”[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** Goal 2 benchmark: “Dictate a complete claim sentence answering the prompt.”
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Oral answer frame on the teacher clipboard.[[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[CHANGED]]Right after paragraph 8 is read.

#### During Reading Question B
- **Original step:** In your own words, explain at least three reasons why Lowe thinks his definition that community is “a shared story” works.
- **Modified step for Marcus:** [[CHANGED]]Have him choose three reasons from a short list and say them aloud before writing or dictating them.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** Accommodation: “Oral responses accepted in place of writing for comprehension checks”; Present level: “Strong oral participation.”
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]3-choice reason bank with these labels: based on place/interest/religion, gives belonging, shapes how people see the world, people can belong to more than one community.[[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[CHANGED]]At the end of paragraph 8, before moving to paragraph 9.

---

### 6) Whole Class Reading — Paragraphs 10–11

- **Original step:** Students find evidence that answers both questions in the title.
- **Modified step for Marcus:** [[CHANGED]]Have Marcus use TTS for paragraphs 10–11, then highlight two lines on the page and explain orally why each one answers a different part of the title.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** Present level: “With text-to-speech, comprehension ~grade level on the same text.”
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Highlight bar with labels: “What is community?” and “Why is it important?”[[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[CHANGED]]During the final reading block, before independent practice.

---

### 7) Independent Practice — Multiple Choice (5 min)

- **Original step:** Students answer four multiple-choice questions.
- **Modified step for Marcus:** [[CHANGED]]Give Marcus the same four questions in the modified version below. Read the directions once aloud, then let him answer independently with headphones on and the answer choices spaced out on the page.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** “Text-to-speech for any text > 1 paragraph”; “Headphones permitted for TTS without singling out”; Present level: “runs out of time before he can demonstrate what he knows.”
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]One-page answer sheet with extra spacing and reduced visual clutter.[[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[CHANGED]]At the start of Independent Practice.

---

### 8) Independent Practice — Short Response (15 min)

- **Original step:** Students write a short response explaining the meaning of the quote.
- **Modified step for Marcus:** [[CHANGED]]Let Marcus dictate the response into speech-to-text first, then do a 2-minute edit with him for punctuation and homophone errors.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** Goal 2 — “Produce a 5-sentence on-grade analytical paragraph using speech-to-text + spell check”; benchmark: “Dictate a complete claim sentence answering the prompt.”
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Sentence-stem scaffold + 5-sentence checklist tied to Goal 2.[[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[CHANGED]]During the full 15-minute short-response block.

---

### 9) Student-Led Discussion (5 min)

- **Original step:** Discuss three questions with a partner and record both answers.
- **Modified step for Marcus:** [[CHANGED]]Keep him with a partner in the whole group. Let him answer the first question orally, choose one question to record using speech-to-text, and skip any requirement to read the partner’s response aloud.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** “Do not pull the student out of whole-group when the IEP places them in inclusion and they prefer whole group”; “Oral responses accepted in place of writing for comprehension checks.”
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Partner discussion card with one recording box already labeled “My answer.”[[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[CHANGED]]During the final 5 minutes, while the class is still with partners.
