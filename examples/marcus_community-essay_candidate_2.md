# Tomorrow's Lesson — Marcus (Grade 7)

## PART A — Teacher Cheat Sheet

### Before the bell
- [[NEW]]Open the lesson text in a TTS-friendly format and test headphones once on Marcus’s device.[[/NEW]]  
  [[WHY]]Goal 3 — Self-advocacy: “Set up TTS on a new device unprompted.” Accommodation: “Text-to-speech for any text > 1 paragraph.” Lesson part: “Whole Class Reading” and “Independent Practice.”[[/WHY]]

- [[NEW]]Put Marcus near the charging outlet and hand him the modified one-page question sheet before the intro starts.[[/NEW]]  
  [[WHY]]Accommodation: “Seated near a charging outlet for assistive device.” Present level: “Without accommodations he runs out of time before he can demonstrate what he knows.” Lesson part: “Intro Slide Deck” and “During Reading Questions.”[[/WHY]]

- [[NEW]]Write two oral vocabulary previews on sticky notes: “community = shared story,” “identity-forming narrative = story that shapes who you are.”[[/NEW]]  
  [[WHY]]Accommodation: “Vocabulary previewed orally before reading.” Goal 2 — Written expression with assistive tech: “Dictate a complete claim sentence answering the prompt.” Lesson part: “Vocabulary” and “Independent Practice short response.”[[/WHY]]

### Watch for
- [[NEW]]He stops tracking the text when the paragraph gets dense or has several commas and “and/but/because” clauses.[[/NEW]]  
  [[WHY]]Present level: “Comprehension drops sharply on multi-clause sentences.” Lesson part: “Paragraphs 1-2” and “Paragraphs 8-11.”[[/WHY]]

- [[NEW]]He starts joking, looking away, or says something self-deprecating when the class gets to any read-aloud or partner reading move.[[/NEW]]  
  [[WHY]]Present level: “Will refuse to read aloud” and “Becomes self-deprecating about reading in front of peers.” Lesson part: “Whole Class Reading” and “Student-Led Discussion.”[[/WHY]]

- [[NEW]]He answers fast and well when the question is spoken, but leaves blank space when he has to hunt for the answer in print alone.[[/NEW]]  
  [[WHY]]Present level: “With text-to-speech, Marcus performs grade-appropriate analytical work; without it, his comprehension collapses.” Lesson part: “During Reading Questions” and “Independent Practice.”[[/WHY]]

### If they disengage
1. [[NEW]]Point to the headphones and say: “Marcus, use your read-aloud now. Start at the next question only.”[[/NEW]]  
2. [[NEW]]Then hand him the modified sheet and say: “You can answer this one out loud to me or type it after you hear it.”[[/NEW]]

[[WHY]]Goal 3 — Self-advocacy: “Independently request and set up his accommodations (TTS, headphones, extra time) in 4 of 5 opportunities.” Accommodation: “Oral responses accepted in place of writing for comprehension checks.” Lesson part: “During Reading Questions” / “Independent Practice.”[[/WHY]]

### Praise specifically
- [[NEW]]“You used the audio and caught the central idea fast — that’s exactly how a strong reader handles a hard text.”[[/NEW]]

- [[NEW]]“That claim is clear and text-based; you pulled the meaning out even when the print was dense.”[[/NEW]]

- [[NEW]]“You handled that without shutting down and asked for what you needed — that’s strong self-advocacy.”[[/NEW]]

## PART B — Modified Lesson Plan

### 1) Intro Slide Deck — 5 min
- Original step: [[SAME]]Guide students through the slide deck to introduce them to the reading lesson and skill focus.[[/SAME]]

- Modified step for Marcus: [[CHANGED]]Keep him in whole group, but while the class hears the title and skill focus, he follows along on the device with TTS turned on for the text title and purpose statement. Give him the two oral vocabulary previews before the class opens the article.[[/CHANGED]]

- IEP citation: [[NEW]]Goal 1 — Reading fluency: “Track text without finger or marker for 1 minute.” Accommodation: “Text-to-speech for any text > 1 paragraph.” “Vocabulary previewed orally before reading.” Present level: “Listening comprehension at G7.”[[/NEW]]

- Exact materials swap or scaffold: [[NEW]]Small sticky-note vocab card with “community = shared story” and “identity-forming narrative = story that shapes who you are.”[[/NEW]]

---

### 2) Whole Class Reading / Paragraphs 1-2 — within 15 min block
- Original step: [[SAME]]Students read and answer the During Reading Questions.[[/SAME]]

- Modified step for Marcus: [[CHANGED]]Have Marcus listen to Paragraphs 1-2 with TTS while the class reads. When the teacher pauses for Question A, Marcus answers orally first, then marks the same answer on his sheet. Do not ask him to read the paragraph aloud.[[/CHANGED]]

- IEP citation: [[NEW]]Accommodation: “Text-to-speech for any text > 1 paragraph,” “Oral responses accepted in place of writing for comprehension checks,” and “Never require unprompted oral reading.” Present level: “With text-to-speech, Marcus performs grade-appropriate analytical work.”[[/NEW]]

- Exact materials swap or scaffold: [[NEW]]Question card with a one-line gloss under A: “What is this question really asking? Tell me what the author says about the word ‘community’ in paragraph 1 and how paragraph 2 adds to it.”[[/NEW]]

---

### 3) Whole Class Reading / Paragraph 2 Bulleted List — within 15 min block
- Original step: [[SAME]]Students read and answer the During Reading Questions.[[/SAME]]

- Modified step for Marcus: [[CHANGED]]Before the class discusses the bulleted list, pause and say the three key traits out loud once, then let Marcus choose which one he wants to point to on the page. He can answer C by speaking a 3-part oral summary instead of writing a full sentence.[[/CHANGED]]

- IEP citation: [[NEW]]Goal 3 — Self-advocacy: “Name his accommodations when asked.” Present level: “Strong oral participation; struggles with dense print.” Accommodation: “Oral responses accepted in place of writing for comprehension checks.”[[/NEW]]

- Exact materials swap or scaffold: [[NEW]]Three-box response frame: “A community can be ___ / Communities can ___ / People can ___.”[[/NEW]]

---

### 4) Partner Reading / Paragraphs 3-7 — within 15 min block
- Original step: [[SAME]]Students read and answer the During Reading Questions.[[/SAME]]

- Modified step for Marcus: [[CHANGED]]Keep him with a partner, but let the partner read the print while Marcus follows with TTS on his own device. Marcus gives the answer to Turn & Talk B verbally first, then the partner records it or Marcus dictates one sentence into speech-to-text.[[/CHANGED]]

- IEP citation: [[NEW]]Goal 2 — Written expression with assistive tech: “Dictate a complete claim sentence answering the prompt.” Accommodation: “Speech-to-text for written responses > 2 sentences.” Present level: “Ideas are strong; spelling and mechanics are well below grade level.”[[/NEW]]

- Exact materials swap or scaffold: [[NEW]]Sentence stem strip: “Lowe’s example shows that a community is a shared story because ___.”[[/NEW]]

---

### 5) Whole Class Reading / Paragraph 8 — within 15 min block
- Original step: [[SAME]]Students read and answer the During Reading Questions.[[/SAME]]

- Modified step for Marcus: [[CHANGED]]After the class hears Paragraph 8, ask Marcus to answer the Turn & Talk in one spoken sentence using the bulleted list in Paragraph 2 as his reference. If he needs a boost, point him back to the two earlier answers he already gave.[[/CHANGED]]

- IEP citation: [[NEW]]Present level: “With text-to-speech, comprehension ~grade level on the same text.” Goal 1 — Reading fluency: “Re-read for fluency after first cold read.”[[/NEW]]

- Exact materials swap or scaffold: [[NEW]]Mini reference box on his page: “Look back: 1) different kinds of communities 2) positive feelings 3) identity/belonging 4) moral force 5) different from other groups 6) many communities.”[[/NEW]]

---

### 6) Whole Class Reading / Paragraphs 9-11 — within 15 min block
- Original step: [[SAME]]Students read and answer the During Reading Questions.[[/SAME]]

- Modified step for Marcus: [[CHANGED]]Have Marcus listen to Paragraphs 9-11 with TTS, then stop after Paragraph 11 and let him give the “Find Evidence” answer orally in two pieces: one quote about what community is and one quote about why it matters.[[/CHANGED]]

- IEP citation: [[NEW]]Goal 2 — Written expression with assistive tech: “Dictate two pieces of supporting evidence with paragraph references.” Accommodation: “Speech-to-text for written responses > 2 sentences.”[[/NEW]]

- Exact materials swap or scaffold: [[NEW]]Evidence picker with two labeled lines: “Community is…” and “Why it matters…”[[/NEW]]

---

### 7) Independent Practice — MCQs — 20 min block
- Original step: [[SAME]]Students complete multiple choice questions and a short answer prompt to measure their understanding of the text.[[/SAME]]

- Modified step for Marcus: [[CHANGED]]Give Marcus the same four MCQs on his modified sheet, but with one-sentence glosses under each question. Read the item aloud only if he asks; otherwise he uses TTS and answers on paper or by pointing before marking. Keep the answers the same and do not reduce the standard.[[/CHANGED]]

- IEP citation: [[NEW]]Accommodation: “Text-to-speech for any text > 1 paragraph,” “Extra time (1.5x) on reading-heavy assessments,” and “Spelling errors not penalized.” Present level: “Runs out of time before he can demonstrate what he knows.”[[/NEW]]

- Exact materials swap or scaffold: [[NEW]]Modified MCQ sheet with glosses:
  - Q1 gloss: “What is the author mainly trying to do?”
  - Q2 gloss: “Which quote best shows the main idea?”
  - Q3 gloss: “What does ‘slighted’ mean here?”
  - Q4 gloss: “How do paragraphs 5-7 help explain the main idea?”[[/NEW]]

---

### 8) Independent Practice — Short Response — 20 min block
- Original step: [[SAME]]Students complete multiple choice questions and a short answer prompt to measure their understanding of the text.[[/SAME]]

- Modified step for Marcus: [[CHANGED]]Let Marcus dictate the short response into speech-to-text first, then do a 60-second partner-free check where he reads it back to himself using TTS or listens to the device read it aloud, and fixes only missing punctuation or clear meaning errors.[[/CHANGED]]

- IEP citation: [[NEW]]Goal 2 — Written expression with assistive tech: “Produce a 5-sentence on-grade analytical paragraph using speech-to-text + spell check, with 4 of 5 sentences free of meaning-altering errors.” Benchmarks: “Dictate a complete claim sentence answering the prompt” and “Dictate two pieces of supporting evidence with paragraph references.”[[/NEW]]

- Exact materials swap or scaffold: [[NEW]]Sentence stems:
  - “Lowe means that a community is…”
  - “One detail from the text is…”
  - “Another detail from the text is…”
  - “This matters because…”[[/NEW]]

---

### 9) Student-Led Discussion — 5 min block
- Original step: [[SAME]]Students practice speaking with academic language as they discuss questions about the text with a partner.[[/SAME]]

- Modified step for Marcus: [[CHANGED]]Keep Marcus in the partner discussion. Before he starts, point to the question and ask him to choose one of two answer paths: speak first, or jot a few words and then speak. Do not ask him to read a turn aloud cold. Let him answer all three questions orally if he wants.[[/CHANGED]]

- IEP citation: [[NEW]]Goal 3 — Self-advocacy: “Politely decline unprompted oral reading without disengaging.” Present level: “Responds well to choice (which scaffold to use) and to being treated as a capable thinker.”[[/NEW]]

- Exact materials swap or scaffold: [[NEW]]Choice strip: “Speak it / type it / point to it first.”[[/NEW]]

[[SAME]]Keep the same standards and answer choices.[[/SAME]]  
[[NEW]]Add these one-sentence “what this question is really asking” lines under each item.[[/NEW]]

#### 1. Which best describes the author’s main purpose in writing the article? [RI.6]
A. to illustrate how important it is to be part of multiple communities  
B. to demonstrate the way community has shaped the author's life  
C. to describe the life of a person who lives in Newcastle  
D. to give a clear and useful definition of community

#### 2. Which piece of evidence best illustrates the central idea of the article? [RI.1]
A. “So, one of the communities that I consider myself to be part of is the community based around the city of Newcastle.” (Paragraph 5)  
B. “It is this choice to participate in the making and remaking of these stories about the city that makes me part of the community of Newcastle.” (Paragraph 7)  
C. “Community is a group of people who share a story that is so important to them that it defines an aspect of who they are.” (Paragraph 9)  
D. “Social change requires that we rewrite our communal narratives.” (Paragraph 11)  

[[NEW]]What this question is really asking: Which quote best proves the main idea?[[/NEW]]

#### 3. Reread the excerpt from paragraph 5:

What is the best meaning of the word “slighted” as it is used in the excerpt? [RI.4]
A. bored  
B. insulted  
C. motivated  
D. recognized  

[[NEW]]What this question is really asking: What does “slighted” mean in this sentence?[[/NEW]]

B. by describing life for members of the Newcastle community  
C. by giving an example of a community that is part of the author’s story
