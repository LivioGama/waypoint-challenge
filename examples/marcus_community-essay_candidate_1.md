# Tomorrow's Lesson — Marcus (Grade 7)

## PART A — Teacher Cheat Sheet

### Before the bell
- [[NEW]]Place Marcus’s printed page packet at his seat: modified MCQs, modified short-response sheet, and the RI.7.2 graphic organizer. Put the headphones and Chromebook/TTS link ready on his desk or charging spot.[[/NEW]]
- [[NEW]]Open the text in a TTS-friendly format so Marcus can listen from the start of the lesson during the whole-class read. This is the fastest way to keep him with the class when the text gets dense or multi-clause.[[/NEW]]
- [[NEW]]Write two sentence starters on the board for discussion and writing: “The central idea is…” and “One detail that shows this is…”.[[/NEW]]

### Watch for
- [[NEW]]He starts rereading the same sentence, loses his place, or stares at a long paragraph during Paragraphs 1–2 or 8–11. That text is packed with multi-clause sentences, which is exactly where his comprehension drops without TTS.[[/NEW]]
- [[NEW]]He jokes, shuts down, or says the work is “too much” when he sees the short response. He is strong verbally but gets stuck when writing is print-heavy or spelling-heavy.[[/NEW]]
- [[NEW]]He avoids any part that asks for reading aloud or cold response. The IEP says he may “refuse to read aloud,” and unprompted oral reading is a confidence trigger for him.[[/NEW]]

### If they disengage
1. [[NEW]]Say: “Marcus, let’s switch to the audio version for this paragraph. You can listen and use the graphic organizer while we all read.”[[/NEW]]
2. [[NEW]]Point to the next exact task and give a choice: “Do you want to answer by speaking it to me or by typing with speech-to-text?” This uses the IEP response supports and gets him back into the lesson without pulling him out.[[/NEW]]

### Praise specifically
- [[NEW]]“You just picked out the central idea from a hard paragraph — that’s exactly what strong readers do.”[[/NEW]]
- [[NEW]]“Your explanation used the text’s own words and made a clear connection. That shows real analytical thinking.”[[/NEW]]
- [[NEW]]“You asked for the support you needed and kept going. That is strong self-advocacy.”[[/NEW]]

## PART B — Modified Lesson Plan

### 1) Intro Slide Deck
- **Original step:** Guide students through the slide deck to introduce them to the reading lesson and skill focus.
- **Modified step for Marcus:** [[CHANGED]]Keep him in the whole-group intro, but pair each key slide with one spoken summary and one on-screen keyword highlight. When the slide says “central idea,” point to the phrase and say, “This means the main message the author wants us to understand.”[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[CHANGED]]Goal 2 — Written expression with assistive tech: “produce a 5-sentence on-grade analytical paragraph using speech-to-text + spell check.” Present level: “With text-to-speech, Marcus performs grade-appropriate analytical work.”[[/CHANGED]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]No extra handout yet. Use the board stems: “The central idea is…” / “One detail is…”.[[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[CHANGED]]During the first 5 minutes, while the class is still on the intro slides.

### 2) Vocabulary Preview
- **Original step:** Vocabulary is introduced in the teacher copy and embedded before reading.
- **Modified step for Marcus:** [[CHANGED]]Say the four words aloud before reading, and have Marcus repeat only the meaning in plain language, not the definition. Then point to the words in the text when they appear.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[CHANGED]]Accommodation: “Vocabulary previewed orally before reading.” Present level: “comprehension drops sharply on multi-clause sentences.”[[/CHANGED]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Mini vocab strip with kid-friendly meanings: aspect = part, moral = right/wrong, narrative = story, specific = exact.[[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[CHANGED]]Right before the reading begins, still in the intro block.

### 3) Whole-Class Reading — Paragraphs 1–2
- **Original step:** Students read the opening together and answer During Reading Questions.
- **Modified step for Marcus:** [[CHANGED]]Let Marcus follow the text with TTS on headphones while the class reads. When you pause for Question A and B, invite him to answer orally first, then have him choose one sentence to type or dictate.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[CHANGED]]Accommodation: “Text-to-speech for any text > 1 paragraph,” “Oral responses accepted in place of writing for comprehension checks,” and “Headphones permitted for TTS without singling out.”[[/CHANGED]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]During Reading Question card with a single-line support: “The claim is…” / “The list matters because…”.[[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[CHANGED]]During the 15-minute During Reading Questions block, at Paragraphs 1–2.

### 4) Whole-Class Reading — Paragraph 2 During Reading Questions
- **Original step:** Students answer questions about the bulleted list in paragraph 2.
- **Modified step for Marcus:** [[CHANGED]]Give Marcus the question card before he has to speak. Ask him to point to the bullet that matches the claim, then say his answer in his own words. If needed, he can use the audio and answer from the organizer instead of rereading the whole paragraph.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[CHANGED]]Goal 1 — Reading fluency benchmark: “Track text without finger or marker for 1 minute.” Present level: “Reading rate ~75 wpm (G7 target ~150).”[[/CHANGED]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]A two-column note box: “Claim” / “Bulleted list shows…”.[[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[CHANGED]]Right after Paragraph 2, before moving on.

### 5) Partner Reading — Paragraphs 3–7
- **Original step:** Students read with a partner and answer questions.
- **Modified step for Marcus:** [[CHANGED]]Keep him in the whole-group/partner rotation, but do not ask him to be the first reader. Let him listen through TTS, then discuss with the partner using the question stem. If the partner reads aloud, Marcus can follow along in the text and stop to mark evidence with a highlighter or finger on the screen.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[CHANGED]]Modification: “Never require unprompted oral reading.” Present level: “He will refuse to read aloud” and “responds well to choice (which scaffold to use).”[[/CHANGED]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Evidence tracker with two boxes: “What Lowe says” / “How this proves community is a shared story.”[[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[CHANGED]]During the partner-reading section, Paragraphs 3–7.

### 6) Turn & Talk — Community Example
- **Original step:** Students share how a community they belong to fits Lowe’s definition.
- **Modified step for Marcus:** [[SAME]]He does the Turn & Talk with a partner in the room.[[/SAME]] [[CHANGED]]Before he speaks, give him 10 seconds to look at the sentence stem and the example box from the organizer. Then have him answer with one community and one shared story detail.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[CHANGED]]Goal 3 — Self-advocacy: “Name his accommodations when asked.” Present level: “Strong oral participation.”[[/CHANGED]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Sentence stem: “One community I belong to is ____. The shared story is ____.”[[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[CHANGED]]After Paragraph 7, during the partner turn-and-talk.

### 7) Whole-Class Reading — Paragraph 8
- **Original step:** Students read the second bulleted list and answer why the author includes it.
- **Modified step for Marcus:** [[CHANGED]]Read the section aloud with TTS, then ask Marcus to sort the ideas into two categories on his organizer: “what the definition explains” and “why the definition works.” He can answer orally if the writing is slowing him down.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[CHANGED]]Accommodation: “Oral responses accepted in place of writing for comprehension checks.” Present level: “Listening comprehension is at or above grade level.”[[/CHANGED]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Two-category sort organizer with the labels already printed.[[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[CHANGED]]In the middle of the During Reading Questions block, at Paragraph 8.

### 8) Whole-Class Reading — Paragraphs 9–11
- **Original step:** Students find evidence that answers the title question.
- **Modified step for Marcus:** [[CHANGED]]Give him the two target lines already boxed in the text copy, then ask him to explain which line answers “what is community” and which line answers “why is it important.” Have him point first, then speak, then write only if he has time.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[CHANGED]]Goal 2 — Written expression with assistive tech benchmark: “Dictate a complete claim sentence answering the prompt.” Present level: “ideas are strong; spelling and mechanics are well below grade level.”[[/CHANGED]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Highlighted-text copy with two boxed evidence lines.[[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[CHANGED]]At the end of the During Reading Questions block, Paragraphs 9–11.

### 9) Independent Practice — Multiple Choice #1–4
- **Original step:** Students complete four MCQs.
- **Modified step for Marcus:** [[CHANGED]]Give him the modified question sheet with shorter wording and the “what this is really asking” line under each question. He still answers the same four questions, but he can mark answers on paper or on the screen, then read back his choice to check it.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[CHANGED]]Accommodation: “Text-to-speech for any text > 1 paragraph,” “Extra time (1.5x) on reading-heavy assessments.” Present level: “Without accommodations he runs out of time before he can demonstrate what he knows.”[[/CHANGED]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Modified MCQ sheet for Independent Practice #1–4.[[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[CHANGED]]At the start of the 20-minute Independent Practice block.

### 10) Independent Practice — Short Response Prompt
- **Original step:** Students answer the short response prompt.
- **Modified step for Marcus:** [[CHANGED]]Have Marcus use speech-to-text for the short response. He should build one claim sentence, then add two details from the text, then check punctuation and homophones before turning it in.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[CHANGED]]Goal 2 — Written expression with assistive tech: “Produce a 5-sentence on-grade analytical paragraph using speech-to-text + spell check, with 4 of 5 sentences free of meaning-altering errors.” Benchmarks: “Dictate a complete claim sentence answering the prompt” and “Dictate two pieces of supporting evidence with paragraph references.”[[/CHANGED]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Short-response scaffold with 5 sentence frames and a 3-step checklist.[[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[CHANGED]]During the short-response portion of Independent Practice.

### 11) Student-Led Discussion
- **Original step:** Students discuss questions with a partner and record both answers.
- **Modified step for Marcus:** [[SAME]]He stays in the partner discussion.[[/SAME]] [[CHANGED]]If the room gets noisy, let him keep headphones on while he looks at the question card. Ask him to use one answer stem aloud and one written line in the recording box.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[CHANGED]]Accommodation: “Headphones permitted for TTS without singling out.” Goal 3 — Self-advocacy benchmark: “Politely decline unprompted oral reading without disengaging.”[[/CHANGED]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Discussion card with the three questions rewritten in plain language and answer stems.[[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[CHANGED]]Final 5 minutes of the lesson.
