# Tomorrow's Lesson — Devon (Grade 7)

## Part A — Teacher Cheat Sheet

### Before the bell
- [[NEW]]Put a one-page writing planner on Devon’s desk before class starts: **claim → 3 reasons → evidence → conclusion**. This matches **Goal 2 — Written expression — at grade level**: **“Use a written planning template (claim → 3 evidence → conclusion) before drafting, 4/5 assignments.”** It fits the lesson’s **Independent Practice** at lines **115–120**. [[/NEW]]
- [[NEW]]Put a sticky note on the lesson handout with the exact place Devon will start: **“Write your issue + position first.”** This matches **Goal 2** and the lesson’s **Independent Practice** lines **115–120**, so he can start writing right away instead of freezing at a blank page. [[/NEW]]
- [[NEW]]Have the red-card break signal visible on his desk and the sentence stem card ready: **“I’m noticing ____. I need my 5-minute break.”** This matches **Goal 1 — Anxiety self-management**: **“deploy a taught coping strategy (box breathing, 5-4-3-2-1 grounding, red-card break, request co-regulation)”** and the accommodation **“'silent signal' (red card on desk) to leave the room without verbal exchange.”** It fits the lesson’s whole-group start at lines **88–99**. [[/NEW]]

### Watch for
- [[NEW]]He goes quiet or stares at the blank page during **Independent Practice** lines **115–120**. For Devon, that usually means the task pressure is rising, not that he doesn’t know the answer. [[/NEW]]
- [[NEW]]He answers the model questions in his head but does not write anything during **Partner Reading** lines **96–113**. That is a common “I know it, but I can’t get started in front of people” move for him. [[/NEW]]
- [[NEW]]He hesitates when the class is asked to talk about a personal or school issue at lines **115–125**. His anxiety triggers include performance pressure and peer attention, and the lesson asks for a public choice of topic. [[/NEW]]

### If they disengage
- [[NEW]]Step 1: Point to the red card or planner and say, **“Write the first box only: issue and position.”** This uses his **Goal 2** benchmark: **“Complete first paragraph in gen ed setting before optional move to alternate setting, 3/5 assignments.”** [[/NEW]]
- [[NEW]]Step 2: If he still freezes, hand him the break card and quietly say, **“Use your silent signal, take 5 minutes, then come back and finish the first box.”** This matches **Goal 1**: **“use a taught coping strategy (box breathing, 5-4-3-2-1 grounding, red-card break, request co-regulation)”** and the accommodation **“5-minute breaks on request, no explanation required.”** [[/NEW]]

### Praise specifically
- “[[NEW]]You wrote a clear position right away in the first box—that’s exactly how strong arguments start.[[/NEW]]”
- “[[NEW]]You found evidence that actually fits your reason, not just any fact. That’s strong persuasive writing.[[/NEW]]”
- “[[NEW]]You kept working in the general classroom even when the task got big. That matches your plan for finishing writing here.[[/NEW]]”

## Part B — Modified Lesson Plan

### Block 1: Introduction to persuasive writing
- **Original step:** [[SAME]]Start with a conversation about times students have been persuaded.[[/SAME]]
- **Modified step for Devon:** [[CHANGED]]Give Devon the written version of the prompt on his desk before the discussion starts: **“Think of a time someone changed your mind. What did they say?”** He may write one word or a full sentence instead of speaking.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[NEW]]Goal 2 — Written expression — at grade level: **“Written response accepted in place of any oral response.”** Goal 1 — Anxiety self-management: **“deploy a taught coping strategy (box breathing, 5-4-3-2-1 grounding, red-card break, request co-regulation)”**[[/NEW]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Sticky-note response strip with 2 sentence stems: **“Someone convinced me when…”** / **“They said…”**[[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[NEW]]At the exact moment the class begins the opening conversation, before any student shares aloud.[[/NEW]]

### Block 2: Vocabulary and model essay setup
- **Original step:** [[SAME]]Read the model essay aloud or have students read it silently, then discuss as a class.[[/SAME]]
- **Modified step for Devon:** [[CHANGED]]Hand Devon a highlighted model essay with four labels in the margin: **thesis, reason, evidence, counterargument**. Ask him to underline the thesis in the first read and circle one piece of evidence in the second read.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[NEW]]Goal 2 — Written expression — at grade level: **“Use a written planning template (claim → 3 evidence → conclusion) before drafting, 4/5 assignments.”** Present level: **“Written expression on grade level when not under time/performance pressure”**[[/NEW]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]One-copy annotated model essay with color key; no oral directions only.[[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[NEW]]During the model-essay read, before partner talk starts.[[/NEW]]

### Block 3: Partner reading questions
- **Original step:** [[SAME]]Work with a partner to analyze the essay using the 3 questions.[[/SAME]]
- **Modified step for Devon:** [[CHANGED]]Let Devon answer the 3 partner questions in writing first, then share only one answer with the partner if he chooses. Keep the partner close, but do not require turn-taking reading aloud.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[NEW]]Accommodations — Presentation: **“Written response accepted in place of any oral response”** and **“No cold-calling — Devon raises hand or signals to participate.”** Present level: **“Strongly prefers writing over speaking.”**[[/NEW]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Mini answer card with the 3 questions rewritten in simpler words; answer lines underneath each.[[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[NEW]]Right when the class moves into Partner Reading, before they discuss question 1.[[/NEW]]

### Block 4: During reading questions A–D
- **Original step:** [[SAME]]Students respond to Think & Share, Turn & Talk, Write, and Find Evidence questions.[[/SAME]]
- **Modified step for Devon:** [[CHANGED]]Require only the writing tasks from A–D on paper: he writes a 1-sentence prediction, a 1-sentence agreement/disagreement, one evidence sentence, and one highlighted counterargument sentence. If the class talks, Devon may keep writing while others talk.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[NEW]]Goal 2 — Written expression — at grade level: **“Written response accepted in place of any oral response.”**[[/NEW]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]A 4-box response sheet labeled **Predict / Agree or Disagree / Evidence / Opposing View**.[[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[NEW]]As each question A–D appears in the lesson, before any whole-group share-out.[[/NEW]]

### Block 5: Independent Practice — choose a social issue
- **Original step:** [[SAME]]Choose a social issue you care about and write the issue, position, three reasons, and evidence.[[/SAME]]
- **Modified step for Devon:** [[CHANGED]]Give Devon a choice board with 6 possible social issues from school/community/global topics. He circles one, writes his position, and fills in the planner box by box. He starts with **issue + position** before moving to reasons.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[NEW]]Goal 2 — Written expression — at grade level: **“Complete first paragraph in gen ed setting before optional move to alternate setting, 3/5 assignments.”** Present level: **“In whole-group setting, often submits blank paper or single sentence.”**[[/NEW]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Choice board + planner with the exact order: **Issue → Position → Reason 1 → Evidence 1 → Reason 2 → Evidence 2 → Reason 3 → Evidence 3**.[[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[NEW]]The minute Independent Practice begins, before any student is asked to brainstorm on their own.[[/NEW]]

### Block 6: Student-led discussion
- **Original step:** [[SAME]]In small groups, share chosen issues and get feedback.[[/SAME]]
- **Modified step for Devon:** [[CHANGED]]Devon may join the small group after he has written at least the **issue + position + one reason**. He shares by reading from his paper or showing it; he does not need to explain verbally beyond that.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[NEW]]Accommodations — Response: **“Written response accepted in place of any oral response”** and **“Option to submit work via shared doc rather than hand in physically.”**[[/NEW]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Feedback slip with 3 checkboxes: **clear position / strong reason / evidence matches**.[[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[NEW]]Only after he finishes the first chunk of Independent Practice and the class moves to group feedback.[[/NEW]]

### Block 7: If anxiety spikes during writing
- **Original step:** [[SAME]]Students keep working through the lesson.[[/SAME]]
- **Modified step for Devon:** [[CHANGED]]If he shows warning signs, point to the planner and say, **“Box 1 only.”** If needed, let him use the red-card silent signal and take a 5-minute break, then return to finish the first box.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[NEW]]Goal 1 — Anxiety self-management: **“independently identify an early warning sign from the personal warning-signs sheet and deploy a taught coping strategy… BEFORE escalation”** and accommodation **“5-minute breaks on request, no explanation required.”**[[/NEW]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Warning-signs sheet in folder + 1-page planner kept open to the next empty box only.[[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[NEW]]Any time he stops writing for more than about 30 seconds, stares at the paper, or starts to withdraw during the independent task.[[/NEW]]

## Part C — Modified Student Materials

### 1) Partner Reading Questions
[[SAME]]**1. What is the writer trying to convince us of?**  
[[NEW]]What this question is really asking: **What does the author want the reader to believe?**  
[[NEW]]Answer choice to look for: **B. A clear statement of the author's position or belief**[[/NEW]]

[[SAME]]**2. What is a counterargument?**  
[[NEW]]What this question is really asking: **What is the opposite opinion the writer talks about?**  
[[NEW]]Answer choice to look for: **B. An opposing point of view that the author addresses**[[/NEW]]

[[SAME]]**3. If you were writing a persuasive essay to your school principal about extending lunch time, which evidence would be MOST credible?**  
[[NEW]]What this question is really asking: **Which choice sounds most trustworthy and backed by facts?**  
[[NEW]]Answer choice to look for: **B. A study showing that students with longer lunch breaks have better afternoon attendance**[[/NEW]]

[[SAME]]**4. Which sentence is the best thesis statement for a persuasive essay?**  
[[NEW]]What this question is really asking: **Which sentence clearly tells the reader what the writer believes?**  
[[NEW]]Answer choice to look for: **B. "Our school should require students to have one free period for homework instead of study hall."**[[/NEW]]

4. Evidence for each reason: _______________
