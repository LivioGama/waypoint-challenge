# Tomorrow's Lesson — Devon (Grade 7)

## PART A — Teacher Cheat Sheet

### Before the bell
- [[NEW]]Put Devon’s modified student page, a pencil, and the lesson graphic organizer at his seat before class starts.[[/NEW]]  
- [[NEW]]Place the red card on his desk and make sure the warning-signs sheet is in the front folder pocket.[[/NEW]]  
- [[NEW]]Pre-mark the 2 places where Devon may need to answer in writing: Independent Practice short response and Student-Led Discussion record sheet.[[/NEW]]  

### Watch for
- [[NEW]]He goes quiet, looks down, rubs his stomach, or freezes right when the class gets to the phrase “identity-forming narrative” or the multiple-choice section with several similar answer choices.[[/NEW]]  
- [[NEW]]He starts erasing a lot, stops after one question, or asks to leave right when the short response asks him to explain an abstract idea in his own words.[/NEW]]  
- [[NEW]]He avoids the discussion page, stares at the floor, or says “I don’t know” when the lesson shifts to partner talk about a community he belongs to.[[/NEW]]  

### If they disengage
Use this exact 2-step recovery script:
1. [[NEW]]Point to the red card or his folder and say, “You can use your break card now. I’ll nod when you’re ready.”[[/NEW]]  
2. [[NEW]]When he returns, say, “Let’s do just the first sentence together from your organizer, then you pick the next detail.”[[/NEW]]  

This uses the IEP’s named support: [[SAME]]“silent signal” (red card on desk) to leave the room without verbal exchange[[/SAME]] and [[SAME]]“5-minute breaks on request, no explanation required”[[/SAME]].

### Praise specifically
- [[NEW]]“You found the author’s main idea and matched it to the right detail — that’s strong reading.”[[/NEW]]  
- [[NEW]]“You kept going even when the words got abstract. That shows real work stamina.”[[/NEW]]  
- [[NEW]]“You explained your thinking in writing instead of freezing up. That fits your strength as a thoughtful writer.”[[/NEW]]

## PART B — Modified Lesson Plan

### 1) Intro Slide Deck — 5 min
- **Original step:** Guide students through the slide deck to introduce them to the reading lesson and skill focus.
- **Modified step for Devon:** [[CHANGED]]Give the slide deck directions in writing on Devon’s copy, and when the class explains the skill aloud, point to the written line on his page instead of repeating it verbally to him alone.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[SAME]]“Directions given in writing whenever oral directions are given”[[SAME]]; Goal 2 — Written expression — at grade level: [[SAME]]“Strong written work when given choice of topic and quiet setting.”[[SAME]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Devon gets a 1-page lesson map with 3 boxes: “main idea,” “supporting details,” and “what the author means by community.”[[/NEW]]

### 2) Vocabulary + Purpose for Reading — inside Intro Slide Deck
- **Original step:** Pronounce vocabulary together as a class.
- **Modified step for Devon:** [[CHANGED]]Have Devon follow along on a small printed vocab strip with the 4 words and student-friendly meanings already beside them; he only has to circle the word that shows up in the text first.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** Goal 2 — Written expression — at grade level: [[SAME]]“Strong written work when given choice of topic and quiet setting.”[[SAME]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Vocab strip with: aspect = part, moral = about right and wrong, narrative = story, specific = exact.[[/NEW]]

### 3) Paragraphs 1–2, Whole Class Reading — 15 min
- **Original step:** Students read and answer the During Reading Questions.
- **Modified step for Devon:** [[CHANGED]]Keep Devon in the whole-group lesson, but give him the reading in shorter chunks on his copy with the matching question printed directly under each chunk; after paragraph 1, he answers only the first question in writing before the class moves on.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[SAME]]“Seat near door for low-profile exit”[[SAME]]; Goal 1 — Anxiety self-management: [[SAME]]“use a taught coping strategy … BEFORE escalation”[[SAME]]; Goal 2 — Written expression — at grade level: [[SAME]]“Use a written planning template (claim → 3 evidence → conclusion) before drafting”[[SAME]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Add a 2-column organizer: left side “What the text says,” right side “What it means.”[[/NEW]]

#### During Reading Question A
- **Original step:** “What claim does Lowe make about the word ‘community’ in paragraph 1?”
- **Modified step for Devon:** [[SAME]][[NEW]]Keep the question the same, but put this sentence under it: “The author says community is powerful, but people often don’t define it clearly.”[[/NEW]][[/SAME]]
- **IEP citation:** Goal 2 — Written expression — at grade level: [[SAME]]“On grade level when not under time/performance pressure”[[SAME]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Answer choice box: “I think the claim is…”[[/NEW]]

#### During Reading Question B
- **Original step:** “What is the relationship between the claim above and the bulleted list in paragraph 2?”
- **Modified step for Devon:** [[CHANGED]]Before he answers, underline the 3 bullets in paragraph 2 together with a highlighter on his copy and have him write one short sentence: “The bullets list the things a good definition must explain.”[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** Goal 2 — Written expression — at grade level: [[SAME]]“Strong written work when given choice of topic and quiet setting.”[[SAME]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Mini sentence stem: “The bulleted list shows ______ because ______.”[[/NEW]]

#### During Reading Question C
- **Original step:** “Summarize at least three key traits of a community.”
- **Modified step for Devon:** [[CHANGED]]Let Devon choose 3 bullet icons on his organizer instead of speaking, then write the 3 traits in short phrases rather than full sentences.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[SAME]]“Written response accepted in place of any oral response”[[SAME]]; Goal 2 — Written expression — at grade level
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Three-icon box: identity, belonging, moral rules.[[/NEW]]

### 4) Partner Reading — Paragraphs 3–7 — 15 min
- **Original step:** Students read and answer the During Reading Questions.
- **Modified step for Devon:** [[CHANGED]]Devon stays with the whole class/partner structure, but he gets advance notice that the turn-and-talk question will be answered first in writing, then shared with one partner if he chooses.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[SAME]]“No cold-calling — Devon raises hand or signals to participate”[[SAME]]; Goal 1 — Anxiety self-management: [[SAME]]“use a taught coping strategy … BEFORE escalation”[[SAME]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Partner share card with two choices: “read my sentence” or “point to my evidence.”[[/NEW]]

#### Paragraph 3–7 Question A
- **Original step:** “How does Lowe’s example of belonging to the Newcastle community support his definition?”
- **Modified step for Devon:** [[CHANGED]]Give Devon a 2-step prompt on his paper: “1) Find the example. 2) Explain how it proves the definition.” He answers in 2 short written sentences.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** Goal 2 — Written expression — at grade level: [[SAME]]“Complete first paragraph in gen ed setting before optional move to alternate setting, 3/5 assignments.”[[SAME]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Sentence stems: “This example supports the definition because…” / “It shows community is…”[[/NEW]]

#### Paragraph 3–7 Question B
- **Original step:** “Consider the communities you are a part of. Share how one of these communities fits Lowe’s definition.”
- **Modified step for Devon:** [[CHANGED]]He may write about a community instead of speaking about it. If he chooses to share, he reads only his written response, not an improvised answer.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[SAME]]“Written response accepted in place of any oral response”[[SAME]]; Goal 1 — Anxiety self-management: [[SAME]]“request co-regulation”[[SAME]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Choice box of possible communities: family, team, club, neighborhood, online group.[[/NEW]]

#### Paragraphs 8–9 Question A
- **Original step:** “Why does the author include another bulleted list in this section?”
- **Modified step for Devon:** [[CHANGED]]Have Devon mark the word “tests” or “checks” in the text margin and then write one sentence explaining that the author is checking his definition against the earlier list.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** Goal 2 — Written expression — at grade level
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Margin note starter: “The author is testing the definition by…”[[/NEW]]

#### Paragraphs 8–9 Question B
- **Original step:** “Explain at least three reasons why Lowe thinks his definition that community is ‘a shared story’ works.”
- **Modified step for Devon:** [[CHANGED]]Reduce the response load by having Devon choose 3 evidence boxes from a checklist and then turn those into 3 short sentences, one per reason.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[SAME]]“Assignment chunking: long tasks broken into segments with check-in after each segment”[[SAME]]; Goal 2 — Written expression — at grade level
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Checklist with 3 boxes: works for different kinds of communities; creates belonging; shapes how people see good/bad.[[/NEW]]

### 5) Independent Practice — 20 min
- **Original step:** Students complete multiple choice questions and a short answer prompt.
- **Modified step for Devon:** [[CHANGED]]Give Devon the MCQs and short response on a separate page with less clutter, larger spacing, and one question at a time covered until he is ready for it.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[SAME]]“Alternate setting … REQUIRED for all formal assessments”[[SAME]] for formal tests; for this practice, Goal 1 — Anxiety self-management and Goal 2 — Written expression.
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]A cover strip that reveals one question at a time.[[/NEW]]

- **IEP citation:** Goal 2 — Written expression — at grade level
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Small reminder box: “Think about the whole article, not one paragraph.”[[/NEW]]

#### MCQ #2
- **Original step:** “Which piece of evidence best illustrates the central idea of the article?”
- **Modified step for Devon:** [[SAME]][[NEW]]Keep the question and answer choices the same, but add a note: “Choose the quote that best matches the big idea about community.”[[/NEW]][[/SAME]]
- **IEP citation:** Goal 2 — Written expression — at grade level
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Underline cue next to each answer choice for the quote part.[[/NEW]]

#### MCQ #3
- **Original step:** “What is the best meaning of the word ‘slighted’ as it is used in the excerpt?”
- **Modified step for Devon:** [[SAME]][[NEW]]Keep the question and choices the same, but add a one-word clue box: “felt ______ when people said bad things.”[[/NEW]][[/SAME]]

#### MCQ #4

- **IEP citation:** Goal 2 — Written expression — at grade level
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Evidence box next to the choices: “example / explanation / opinion.”[[/NEW]]

#### Short Response Prompt
- **Original step:** “Explain what Lowe means when he says a community is ‘a group of people who share an identity-forming narrative.’”
- **Modified step for Devon:** [[CHANGED]]Keep the prompt exactly the same, but add a 4-line response frame and a checklist that matches Goal 2; he writes in the gen ed room, and if he starts to freeze, he may complete just the first paragraph here and finish the last sentence after a 5-minute break.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[SAME]]Goal 2 — Written expression — at grade level: [[SAME]]“Use a written planning template (claim → 3 evidence → conclusion) before drafting, 4/5 assignments.”[[SAME]]; [[SAME]]“5-minute breaks on request, no explanation required”[[SAME]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Response frame:

### 6) Student-Led Discussion — 5 min
- **Original step:** Students discuss the questions with a partner.
- **Modified step for Devon:** [[CHANGED]]Devon answers on paper first, then may read one prepared sentence to a partner; he is not required to improvise, and he may point to his answer instead of speaking if anxious.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[SAME]]“Written response accepted in place of any oral response”[[SAME]]; Goal 1 — Anxiety self-management: [[SAME]]“request co-regulation”[[SAME]]; present level: [[SAME]]“Self-aware, articulate about his anxiety in 1:1 settings”[[SAME]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Partner discussion card with check boxes: “I wrote my answer,” “I shared one sentence,” “I pointed to my evidence.”[[/NEW]]
