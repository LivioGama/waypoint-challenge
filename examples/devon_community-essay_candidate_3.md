# Tomorrow's Lesson — Devon (Grade 7)

## PART A — Teacher Cheat Sheet

### Before the bell
- [[NEW]]Put Devon’s modified packet on his desk before students enter: the “community” text, the 4 MCQs, the short-response page, and the RI.7.2 graphic organizer.[[/NEW]]
- [[CHANGED]]Pre-load one written direction on the board for the whole class and for Devon: “Read the sentence, then answer in the box.” This avoids relying on oral directions.[[/CHANGED]]
- [[NEW]]Place the red card on Devon’s desk and put the warning-signs sheet in the front of his folder so he can use them without asking.[[/NEW]]

### Watch for
- [[NEW]]He freezes on the first paragraph or stops moving when the lesson shifts from reading to questions — this text asks him to explain ideas, which can feel like “getting it wrong in public.”[[/NEW]]
- [[NEW]]He stares at the MCQ page but does not start, especially if he thinks he has to answer fast or perfectly.[[/NEW]]
- [[NEW]]He rubs his stomach, tears up, or repeatedly asks to leave right after the short-response prompt appears, because the open response is the most performance-heavy part of today’s lesson.[[/NEW]]

### If they disengage
1. [[CHANGED]]Point to the red card or say, “You can use your break card,” and nod — no extra talk.[[/CHANGED]]
2. [[CHANGED]]When Devon returns, use the exact BIP script: “Take 3 box breaths with me, then do just the next box.”[[/CHANGED]]

### Praise specifically
- [[NEW]]“You found the central idea in paragraph 3 and kept going — that’s strong reading.”[[/NEW]]
- [[NEW]]“You answered in writing instead of freezing up, and your evidence matches the text.”[[/NEW]]
- [[NEW]]“You used the break system and came back to finish the next part — that’s exactly the kind of self-management that helps you learn.”[[/NEW]]

## PART B — Modified Lesson Plan

### 1) Intro Slide Deck — 5 min

- **Original step:** Guide students through the slide deck to introduce them to the reading lesson and skill focus.
- **Modified step for Devon:** [[CHANGED]]Start with the whole class slide deck, but put the skill target in writing on Devon’s paper: “I can find the central idea and details that support it.” Have him whisper-read it once to himself before reading begins.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** Goal 2 — Written expression — at grade level: “complete and submit grade-level written responses”; Present Level: “strong written work when given choice of topic and quiet setting.”
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Add a 1-line focus box at the top of Devon’s packet: “Central idea = what the text is mostly saying.”[[/NEW]]

### 2) Vocabulary preview — built into the deck, 1 min inside intro

- **Original step:** Let students pronounce vocabulary words together as a class.
- **Modified step for Devon:** [[CHANGED]]Let him follow along silently; do not call on him to pronounce vocabulary words aloud. Point to the words on his paper if the class reads them together.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** Accommodations — “No cold-calling — Devon raises hand or signals to participate”; “Written response accepted in place of any oral response.”
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Underline the key vocabulary in Devon’s copy and put a 3-word gloss beside each: aspect = part, narrative = story, specific = exact.[[/NEW]]

### 3) Whole-class reading, Paragraphs 1–2 — about 5 min

- **Original step:** Whole-class reading with During Reading Questions A, B, C.
- **Modified step for Devon:** [[CHANGED]]Keep Devon in whole group. After paragraph 1, pause and have him answer Question A in writing on his sheet before any partner talk. Skip the optional asterisk question if he starts to shut down.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** Goal 2 — benchmark: “Use a written planning template (claim → 3 evidence → conclusion) before drafting”; Accommodation: “Written response accepted in place of any oral response.”
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]For Question A, give a sentence stem: “Lowe says community __________ because __________.”[[/NEW]]

### 4) Paragraph 2 bulleted list — 3 min

- **Original step:** Students read the bulleted list and answer Question B.
- **Modified step for Devon:** [[CHANGED]]Before the class turns and talks, have Devon underline three phrases in the bullets and write one short note beside each. Then he can participate only by sharing if he wants to; no public speaking is required.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** Accommodations — “Written response accepted in place of any oral response”; “No cold-calling — Devon raises hand or signals to participate.”
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Add a mini-box under the bullets titled “3 key traits” with three lines labeled: kinds of communities / feelings / moral choices.[[/NEW]]

### 5) Partner Reading — Paragraphs 3–7 — 7 min

- **Original step:** Partner reading with During Reading Questions A and B.
- **Modified step for Devon:** [[CHANGED]]Let Devon read silently while the partner reads aloud, or let him alternate by paragraph only if he volunteers. Give him the written question for Paragraph 3 before the partner talk so he can write first, talk second.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** Present Level: “Strongly prefers writing over speaking.” Accommodation: “Written response accepted in place of any oral response”; “Directions given in writing whenever oral directions are given.”
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Add a 2-column note box: “What Lowe says” / “How this shows up in Newcastle.”[[/NEW]]

### 6) Whole-class reading, Paragraph 8 — 5 min

- **Original step:** Whole-class reading with During Reading Question A and the author’s second bulleted list.
- **Modified step for Devon:** [[CHANGED]]Hand Devon a 6-line checklist before paragraph 8: “Does this definition fit all communities? Does it explain feelings? Does it explain belonging? Does it explain good/bad? Is it different from family/society? Can people belong to more than one?” He checks boxes as the class reads.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** Goal 2 — benchmark: “Use a written planning template (claim → 3 evidence → conclusion) before drafting.” Present Level: “Thoughtful, articulate in writing.”
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Give him a short “test the definition” organizer with the six criteria already written in student-friendly language.[[/NEW]]

### 7) Paragraphs 9–11 / end of text — 4 min

- **Original step:** Read the final paragraphs and answer the evidence question about why the title matters.
- **Modified step for Devon:** [[CHANGED]]Have Devon highlight two sentences only, not the whole page. Ask him to choose one sentence that answers “what is community?” and one that answers “why does it matter?”[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** Accommodation: “Typed response accepted for any handwritten task”; Goal 2 — benchmark: “Respond in writing to teacher feedback within 48 hours, 4/5 instances.”
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Add two colored boxes at the bottom of the text: yellow = “what it is,” blue = “why it matters.”[[/NEW]]

### 8) Independent Practice — MCQs — 5 min

- **Original step:** Students answer multiple-choice questions.
- **Modified step for Devon:** [[CHANGED]]Give Devon the same 4 questions, but on a clean page with one question per box and the “what this is really asking” line under each. Let him mark answers in pencil or type them.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** Accommodation: “Typed response accepted for any handwritten task”; “Errors not marked in red ink.” Goal 2 — written expression at grade level.
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Attach a tiny text evidence strip at the bottom of the page so he can copy a short phrase from the article if needed.[[/NEW]]

### 9) Independent Practice — short response — 15 min

- **Original step:** Students answer the short response prompt.
- **Modified step for Devon:** [[CHANGED]]Keep the prompt exactly the same, but give Devon a planning box first: claim sentence, 2 details from the text, and a final wrap-up sentence. After he fills the boxes, he turns those notes into a paragraph.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** Goal 2 — benchmark: “Use a written planning template (claim → 3 evidence → conclusion) before drafting, 4/5 assignments.” Accommodation: “Typed response accepted for any handwritten task.”
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Add sentence stems on his page: “Lowe means…” “One detail from the text is…” “Another detail is…” “This shows…”[[/NEW]]

### 10) Student-Led Discussion — 5 min

- **Original step:** Students discuss the questions with a partner and record both answers.
- **Modified step for Devon:** [[CHANGED]]Keep Devon in the partner discussion, but let him write his answer first and let his partner speak more if he chooses. He records his own response in writing, not out loud.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** Accommodation: “Written response accepted in place of any oral response”; “No cold-calling — Devon raises hand or signals to participate.”
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Give him a discussion card with one optional starter: “In my community, courteous behavior is…”[[/NEW]]

### 11) Exit / end of lesson

- **Original step:** Lesson ends after discussion.
- **Modified step for Devon:** [[NEW]]Before he leaves, ask him to circle one part of the lesson he completed and rate his anxiety 1–10 on the tracking line in his packet.[[/NEW]]
- **IEP citation:** Goal 1 — benchmark: “Self-rate anxiety 1–10 at end of day on tracking log, 4/5 days.”
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Add a one-line end-of-lesson check: “I used a strategy when I felt stuck: yes / not yet.”[[/NEW]]
