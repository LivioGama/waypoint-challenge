## PART B — Modified Lesson Plan

### 1) Whole Class Reading
- **Original step:** “Today we're going to start by looking at what makes an argument persuasive. Turn to your neighbor and talk about a time when someone convinced you to change your mind about something.”
- **Modified step for Marcus:** [[CHANGED]]Keep Marcus in the whole-group discussion, but give him a quick choice card with two options: “Tell about a time someone changed your mind” or “Tell about a time you changed someone else’s mind.” He can answer orally to his partner first, then share one sentence with the class if he wants. [[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** Goal 3 — Self-advocacy: “Politely decline unprompted oral reading without disengaging.” Present Levels — “Responds well to choice (which scaffold to use)” and “Becomes self-deprecating about reading in front of peers.”
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Choice card with two talk prompts; no new reading load. [[/NEW]]
- **When:** At the very start of the lesson, before partner talk.

### 2) Model Essay Introduction
- **Original step:** “Now, let's look at a model persuasive essay. Pay attention to: the clear statement of what the writer believes, the reasons and evidence they provide, how they handle disagreement.”
- **Modified step for Marcus:** [[CHANGED]]Play the model essay with TTS or have Marcus follow along in audio while the teacher pauses after each highlighted section and says the three focus points aloud. [[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** Present Levels — “With text-to-speech, comprehension ~grade level on the same text.” Accommodation — “Text-to-speech for any text > 1 paragraph.” Goal 1 — Reading fluency benchmark: “Track text without finger or marker for 1 minute.”
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Highlighted copy with three color marks: thesis, evidence, counterargument. [[/NEW]]
- **When:** During the model essay reading, before discussion starts.

### 3) Partner Reading Questions
- **Original step:** “Work with a partner to analyze the essay. Answer these questions together: 1. What is the writer trying to convince us of? 2. What evidence do they use? 3. Is their argument convincing? Why or why not?”
- **Modified step for Marcus:** [[CHANGED]]Give Marcus the partner questions one at a time. He answers each one orally first; his partner writes if needed, or he can dictate into speech-to-text for Question 2 and 3. [[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** Goal 2 — Written expression with assistive tech: “Dictate a complete claim sentence answering the prompt” and “Dictate two pieces of supporting evidence with paragraph references.” Accommodation — “Speech-to-text for written responses > 2 sentences.”
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Question card with a one-line stem under each question: “The writer is trying to convince us that ___.” “One piece of evidence is ___.” “I think it is convincing because ___.” [[/NEW]]
- **When:** During partner reading, immediately after the teacher finishes the model essay.

### 4) During Reading Questions A–D
- **Original step:** “A. Think & Share After reading the introduction, what do you think this essay will argue? B. Turn & Talk Do you agree or disagree with the writer's main point so far? C. Write Jot down one piece of evidence the writer uses and whether it supports their claim. D. Find Evidence Highlight one sentence where the writer acknowledges a different point of view.”
- **Modified step for Marcus:** [[CHANGED]]Ask Marcus to answer A and B out loud with a partner, then use speech-to-text for C and D so he can keep the focus on ideas instead of spelling and mechanics. [[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** Goal 2 — “Produce a 5-sentence on-grade analytical paragraph using speech-to-text + spell check” and benchmark “Dictate two pieces of supporting evidence with paragraph references.” Accommodation — “Oral responses accepted in place of writing for comprehension checks.”
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Mini response strip with four labeled boxes: Think, Agree/Disagree, Evidence, Counterpoint. [[/NEW]]
- **When:** Right after the teacher reads each question aloud.

### 5) Independent Practice
- **Original step:** “Now it's your turn. Choose a social issue you care about… Write down: 1. The issue 2. Your position 3. Three reasons 4. Evidence for each reason.”
- **Modified step for Marcus:** [[CHANGED]]Keep the same task and same expectation, but narrow the first move: Marcus picks one issue from a teacher-provided list of three school/community topics, then fills the organizer by dictating his claim sentence first and one reason at a time. [[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** Present Levels — “ideas are strong; spelling and mechanics are well below grade level.” Goal 2 — “Dictate a complete claim sentence answering the prompt.” Lesson note — “Support students in finding credible evidence for their claims.”
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Choice list of 3 issues + 4-box organizer with a claim line, reason lines, and evidence lines; sentence stem: “Our school should ___ because ___.” [[/NEW]]
- **When:** At the start of independent practice, before anyone begins writing.

### 6) Student-Led Discussion
- **Original step:** “In small groups, share your chosen issues. Help each other by asking: Is your position clear? Are your reasons strong? What evidence could support this better?”
- **Modified step for Marcus:** [[SAME]]Marcus stays in the small-group discussion with everyone else. [[/SAME]] [[NEW]]Give him one discussion role card: “Ask one question” or “Read one sentence from your organizer.” [[/NEW]]
- **IEP citation:** Goal 3 — “Independently request and set up his accommodations (TTS, headphones, extra time)” and benchmark “Politely decline unprompted oral reading without disengaging.” Present Levels — “Generally engaged and well-liked.”
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Role card with one prompt and one backup sentence stem: “I want to add ___.” [[/NEW]]
- **When:** During the small-group share at the end of class.

### 7) Built-in writing support for the final share
- **Original step:** No separate step; students discuss and give feedback.
- **Modified step for Marcus:** [[NEW]]Before the group feedback starts, have Marcus read or dictate just his thesis sentence and one evidence sentence so he can get feedback on the exact part of the standard that matters most today: claim + evidence. [[/NEW]]
- **IEP citation:** Goal 2 — benchmark “Dictate a complete claim sentence answering the prompt” and “Dictate two pieces of supporting evidence with paragraph references.”
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Two-line feedback box labeled “Claim” and “Evidence.” [[/NEW]]
- **When:** Right before peer feedback begins.

## PART C — Modified Student Materials

**[[CHANGED]]1. What is the writer trying to convince us of?[[/CHANGED]]**  
[[NEW]]What this question is really asking: What is the writer’s main claim?[[/NEW]]  
[[NEW]]Sentence stem: “The writer is trying to convince us that ___.”[[/NEW]]

**[[CHANGED]]2. What evidence do they use?[[/CHANGED]]**  
[[NEW]]What this question is really asking: What facts, examples, or details support the claim?[[/NEW]]  
[[NEW]]Sentence stem: “One piece of evidence they use is ___.”[[/NEW]]

**[[CHANGED]]3. Is their argument convincing? Why or why not?[[/CHANGED]]**  
[[NEW]]What this question is really asking: Do the reasons actually prove the point?[[/NEW]]

### B. During Reading Questions
**[[CHANGED]]A. After reading the introduction, what do you think this essay will argue?[[/CHANGED]]**  
[[NEW]]What this question is really asking: Predict the main claim. [[/NEW]]  
[[NEW]]Sentence stem: “I think the essay will argue that ___.”[[/NEW]]

**[[CHANGED]]B. Do you agree or disagree with the writer's main point so far?[[/CHANGED]]**  
[[NEW]]What this question is really asking: Say whether the claim makes sense to you. [[/NEW]]  
[[NEW]]Sentence stem: “I agree/disagree because ___.”[[/NEW]]

**[[CHANGED]]C. Jot down one piece of evidence the writer uses and whether it supports their claim.[[/CHANGED]]**  
[[NEW]]What this question is really asking: Name one detail and explain how it helps the claim. [[/NEW]]  
[[NEW]]Sentence stem: “The evidence is ___, and it supports the claim because ___.”[[/NEW]]

**[[CHANGED]]D. Highlight one sentence where the writer acknowledges a different point of view.[[/CHANGED]]**  
[[NEW]]What this question is really asking: Find the counterargument. [[/NEW]]  
[[NEW]]Sentence stem: “The counterargument is ___.”[[/NEW]]
