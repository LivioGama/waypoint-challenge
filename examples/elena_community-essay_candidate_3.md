# Tomorrow's Lesson — Elena (Grade 7)

## Part A — Teacher Cheat Sheet

### Before the bell
- [[NEW]]Put Elena’s visual schedule at her desk: **Intro slide deck → whole-class reading → partner reading → Independent Practice → partner discussion**. [[/NEW]]
- [[NEW]]Seat her away from the door, pencil sharpener, and any flicker from lights; place her headphones and a copy of the sentence-frame organizer on her desk before students enter. [[/NEW]]
- [[NEW]]Pre-mark the 3 spots where she will need help most: **Paragraph 2 bulleted list**, **Independent Practice MCQ #2 and #4**, and the **short response prompt**. [[/NEW]]

### Watch for
- [[NEW]]She starts interpreting a line too literally, especially “shared story,” “identity-forming narrative,” or “community is not, in and of itself, morally praiseworthy.” [[/NEW]]
- [[NEW]]She stops writing or rereads the same sentence twice after the lesson shifts from the paragraph text to the bullet list in Paragraph 2. [[/NEW]]
- [[NEW]]She gets quieter, rigid, or tense during the partner talk if the partner speaks fast, changes the topic, or doesn’t stick to the question. [[/NEW]]

### If they disengage
1. [[NEW]]Say: “Elena, use your cue card: **What do you need right now — headphones, a 3-minute break, or the safe-space pass?**” [[/NEW]]
2. [[NEW]]If she doesn’t answer in 10 seconds, point to the card and say: “You can say, **‘I need a minute’** or **‘Wait, did you mean that literally?’** Then take the support.” [[/NEW]]

### Praise specifically
- [[NEW]]“You matched the author’s **claim** to the **bullet points** in Paragraph 2 — that shows strong text tracking.” [[/NEW]]
- [[NEW]]“You used the clue-to-guess-check frame on that inference question, and you stayed with the evidence instead of guessing fast.” [[/NEW]]
- [[NEW]]“You gave a precise written answer in discussion, and that fit the question even better than speaking out loud would have.” [[/NEW]]

## Part B — Modified Lesson Plan

### 1) Intro Slide Deck — 5 min
- **Original step:** Guide students through the slide deck to introduce them to the reading lesson and skill focus.
- **Modified step for Elena (Grade 7):** [[CHANGED]]Keep the class whole-group, but hand Elena the one-page visual lesson map before you speak and point to each part as you name it: **purpose, vocabulary, reading, questions, practice, discussion**. Say the target in plain language: “Today we are figuring out the main idea of the article and the details that prove it.”[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[NEW]]Goal 3 — ELA — Figurative language and inference: baseline says “Inferential and figurative-language items… are answered literally,” and accommodation says “Directions given in writing AND orally; no idioms in directions.”[[/NEW]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Visual lesson map + 3-word preview strip: **claim / evidence / central idea**. [[/NEW]]

### 2) Vocabulary and Purpose for Reading — embedded in intro
- **Original step:** Vocabulary: aspect, moral, narrative, specific.
- **Modified step for Elena (Grade 7):** [[CHANGED]]Before the class reads, give Elena a tiny margin note beside each word with a kid-friendly meaning and one example from school life: **narrative = story**, **specific = exact**, **moral = about right and wrong**, **aspect = part**. Have her circle only the word that matches the sentence you read aloud. [[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[NEW]]Goal 3 — ELA — Figurative language and inference: benchmark “Distinguish literal vs figurative meaning of a target sentence in a grade-level passage, 4/5 items.”[[/NEW]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]4-word vocabulary strip with examples; no reduction of text, just meaning support. [[/NEW]]

### 3) Whole Class Reading — Paragraphs 1–2 — 7 min
- **Original step:** Whole-class reading with During Reading Questions A, B, C.
- **Modified step for Elena (Grade 7):** [[CHANGED]]Read Paragraph 1 aloud to the class, then pause and have Elena follow along with her finger while you ask **Question A**. For **Paragraph 2**, point to each bullet one at a time and ask Elena to mark the bullet that matches the author’s claim with a checkmark, then say the answer in a sentence frame. [[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[NEW]]Goal 3 — ELA — Figurative language and inference: present level says “With visual scaffold accuracy rises to 75–85% on inference items when given a sentence-frame organizer with explicit ‘clue → guess → check’ steps.”[[/NEW]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Sentence frame for response: “The claim is ___. One bullet that matches is ___. This matters because ___.” [[/NEW]]

### 4) During Reading Question B — Paragraph 2 bulleted list — 3 min
- **Original step:** *B. Write: What is the relationship between the claim above and the bulleted list in paragraph 2?*
- **Modified step for Elena (Grade 7):** [[CHANGED]]Do not ask for a general explanation first. Have Elena underline the claim in Paragraph 1, then draw an arrow from that claim to one bullet in Paragraph 2, and finally explain the connection using the frame: “The claim says ___. The bullet list shows ___.”[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[NEW]]Goal 3 — ELA — Figurative language and inference: benchmark “Identify character motivation using a 3-step text-evidence frame, 4/5 prompts,” plus accommodation “Sentence-frame organizers for inference and figurative-language items.”[[/NEW]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Mini organizer with boxes: **claim → matching bullet → why it matches**. [[/NEW]]

### 5) Partner Reading — Paragraphs 3–7 — 8 min
- **Original step:** Partner reading.
- **Modified step for Elena (Grade 7):** [[CHANGED]]Keep her in the whole-group room and let her read silently while the teacher or a strong reader reads the passage aloud. Give her a role card that says **“evidence finder”** so she only has to point to the sentence that proves the answer when the class stops for Question A or B. [[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[NEW]]Goal 1 — Pragmatic communication / Self-regulation: present level says she “prefers parallel play and structured cooperative tasks with assigned roles,” and accommodation says “Assigned role in every group task (recorder, timekeeper, materials manager).”[[/NEW]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Role card: **evidence finder** + highlighter strip for one proof sentence. [[/NEW]]

### 6) During Reading Question A — Paragraphs 3–7 — 4 min
- **Original step:** *A. Write: How does Lowe’s example of belonging to the Newcastle community support his definition?*
- **Modified step for Elena (Grade 7):** [[CHANGED]]Ask her to answer in writing instead of out loud. She circles the sentence about Newcastle, then writes one sentence: “This supports the definition because ___.” [[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[NEW]]Goal 3 — ELA — figurative language/inference; accommodation says “Written response accepted in place of oral participation for whole-group share-outs.”[[/NEW]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]One-sentence response frame plus a circle-the-proof box. [[/NEW]]

### 7) Whole Class Reading — Paragraph 8 — 6 min
- **Original step:** Whole-class reading with Question A and B.
- **Modified step for Elena (Grade 7):** [[CHANGED]]Pause before Paragraph 8’s second bullet list and tell Elena, “This part is testing the author’s definition against the earlier list.” Have her count the new bullets with her finger and label them 1–6 in the margin so she can see the pattern. [[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[NEW]]Goal 2 — Mathematics — Extension / Advanced reasoning: benchmark “justify a solution path in writing using precise mathematical vocabulary” fits her strength with patterning and explicit structure, and present level notes “exceptional pattern recognition.”[[/NEW]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Number labels 1–6 in the margin to reduce unstructured scanning. [[/NEW]]

### 8) During Reading Question A — Paragraph 8 — 3 min
- **Original step:** *A. Turn & Talk: Refer back to the bulleted list in Paragraph 2. Why does the author include another bulleted list in this section?*
- **Modified step for Elena (Grade 7):** [[CHANGED]]Let Elena write her answer first, then turn and share it with one partner using the exact stem: “The author includes another list to ___.” If the partner drifts, redirect Elena to keep her answer tied to the words “tests his definition.”[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[NEW]]Goal 1 — Pragmatic communication / Self-regulation: benchmark “Use a taught repair phrase after a peer interaction breakdown, 3/5 opps with fading prompts.”[[/NEW]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Partner-talk card with two lines: **My answer** / **Partner answer** and the stem already started. [[/NEW]]

### 9) During Reading Question B — Paragraph 8 — 2 min
- **Original step:** *B. Write: In your own words, explain at least three reasons why Lowe thinks his definition that community is “a shared story” works.*
- **Modified step for Elena (Grade 7):** [[CHANGED]]Give Elena a 3-box organizer: **reason 1 / reason 2 / reason 3**. She may copy exact phrases from the text into each box and then add one word of her own to connect them. [[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[NEW]]Goal 3 — ELA — benchmark “Generate one inference per paragraph during guided reading, with text evidence cited.”[[/NEW]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]3-box evidence organizer. [[/NEW]]

### 10) Whole Class Reading — Paragraphs 10–11 — 3 min
- **Original step:** Whole-class reading.
- **Modified step for Elena (Grade 7):** [[CHANGED]]Before reading Paragraph 11, tell Elena exactly what to listen for: “Find the sentence that explains why community matters.” Then have her underline only one sentence in Paragraph 11 and one in Paragraph 9. [[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[NEW]]Goal 3 — ELA — benchmark “Distinguish literal vs figurative meaning of a target sentence in a grade-level passage, 4/5 items,” and present level “literal comprehension is strong.”[[/NEW]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Two underline boxes labeled **title question** and **answer sentence**. [[/NEW]]

### 11) During Reading Question — Paragraphs 10–11 — 2 min
- **Original step:** *Find Evidence: The title of this text is “What is ‘community’ and why is it important?” Highlight two pieces of evidence that answer both questions.*
- **Modified step for Elena (Grade 7):** [[CHANGED]]Have Elena highlight exactly two sentences: one that answers **what community is** and one that answers **why it is important**. Then she labels each highlight with a sticky note: **what** / **why**. [[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[NEW]]Goal 3 — ELA — benchmark “Identify character motivation using a 3-step text-evidence frame, 4/5 prompts.”[[/NEW]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Two sticky notes: **what** and **why**. [[/NEW]]

### 12) Independent Practice — MCQ #1–4 — 8 min
- **Original step:** Students answer the multiple choice questions.
- **Modified step for Elena (Grade 7):** [[CHANGED]]Give Elena the same four questions, but on a version with one bolded line under each item: **What this question is really asking:** ____. She answers in the margin first, then bubbles the choice. [[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[NEW]]Goal 3 — ELA — baseline says inferential items are answered literally, and accommodation says “No ‘cold call’ on inference questions without 30-second think-time and access to organizer.”[[/NEW]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Student copy with “really asking” gloss under each MCQ and a tiny note space. [[/NEW]]

#### MCQ #1
- **Original item:** Which best describes the author’s main purpose in writing the article?
- **Modified student-facing item:** [[CHANGED]]Which answer best tells why the author wrote this article?[[/CHANGED]]
  - A. to show why it matters to belong to more than one community
  - B. to show how community shaped the author’s life
  - C. to describe the life of a person who lives in Newcastle
  - D. to give a clear and useful definition of community
- [[NEW]]What this question is really asking: Which choice names the author’s main point for writing?[[/NEW]]

#### MCQ #2
- **Original item:** Which piece of evidence best illustrates the central idea of the article?
- **Modified student-facing item:** [[CHANGED]]Which quote best shows the article’s central idea?[[/CHANGED]]
  - A. “So, one of the communities that I consider myself to be part of is the community based around the city of Newcastle.”
  - B. “It is this choice to participate in the making and remaking of these stories about the city that makes me part of the community of Newcastle.”
  - C. “Community is a group of people who share a story that is so important to them that it defines an aspect of who they are.”
  - D. “Social change requires that we rewrite our communal narratives.”
- [[NEW]]What this question is really asking: Which quote states the main idea most directly?[[/NEW]]

#### MCQ #3
- **Original item:** What is the best meaning of the word “slighted” as it is used in the excerpt?
- **Modified student-facing item:** [[CHANGED]]What does the word “slighted” mean in this sentence?[[/CHANGED]]
  - A. bored
  - B. insulted
  - C. motivated
  - D. recognized
- [[NEW]]What this question is really asking: Which choice means “felt hurt or offended”?[[/NEW]]

#### MCQ #4
- **Original item:** How do paragraphs 5–7 help the reader understand the author’s central idea?
- **Modified student-facing item:** [[CHANGED]]How do paragraphs 5–7 help explain the main idea?[[/CHANGED]]
  - A. by explaining why community is neither good nor bad
  - B. by describing life for members of the Newcastle community
  - C. by giving an example of a community that is part of the author’s story
  - D. by illustrating how the Newcastle community has many different members
- [[NEW]]What this question is really asking: Which choice says these paragraphs give an example that proves the main idea?[[/NEW]]

### 13) Independent Practice — Short Response — 10 min
- **Original step:** Students respond to the prompt.
- **Modified step for Elena (Grade 7):** [[CHANGED]]Keep the prompt exactly the same, but hand Elena a response box with a sentence-stem starter and a 3-item checklist tied to the evidence requirement. Let her type if she starts to stall on handwriting. [[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[NEW]]Goal 3 — ELA — benchmark “Generate one inference per paragraph during guided reading, with text evidence cited,” plus accommodation “Permission to type rather than handwrite when fine-motor fatigue triggers regulation issues.”[[/NEW]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Typed-response option + sentence stems + checklist. [[/NEW]]

#### Short response prompt
- **Original prompt:** Explain what Lowe means when he says a community is “a group of people who share an identity-forming narrative.”
- **Modified student-facing prompt:** [[SAME]]Explain what Lowe means when he says a community is “a group of people who share an identity-forming narrative.”[[/SAME]]
- [[NEW]]Sentence stems: “Lowe means that a community is ___. One detail from the text is ___. Another detail is ___. This shows that ___.”[[/NEW]]
- [[NEW]]Checklist mapped to Goal 3 benchmark: ☐ I explained the meaning in my own words. ☐ I used 2 details from the text. ☐ I used at least 1 unit word: community, identity, narrative, identity-forming.[[/NEW]]

### 14) Student-Led Discussion — 5 min
- **Original step:** Students discuss the questions with a partner.
- **Modified step for Elena (Grade 7):** [[CHANGED]]Keep Elena in the whole-group room and pair her with a steady partner. Give her the exact order of the three questions and a 10-second think time before she speaks. If speaking stalls, she may point to her written answer instead of repeating it out loud. [[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[NEW]]Goal 1 — Pragmatic communication / Self-regulation: benchmark “Use a taught repair phrase after a peer interaction breakdown, 3/5 opps with fading prompts,” and accommodation “Written response accepted in place of oral participation for whole-group share-outs.”[[/NEW]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Discussion card with numbered questions and answer boxes. [[/NEW]]

#### Discussion Question 1
- **Original item:** Consider a community you are part of. What is something that is considered courteous behavior in that community?
- **Modified student-facing item:** [[CHANGED]]Think of one community you are part of. What is one behavior that is seen as polite in that community?[[/CHANGED]]
- [[NEW]]What this question is really asking: What rule or habit shows you belong there?[[/NEW]]

#### Discussion Question 2
- **Original item:** Do you think it is possible to be a part of a community without conforming to its views? Explain your answer.
- **Modified student-facing item:** [[CHANGED]]Can someone belong to a community even if they do not agree with every view in it? Explain your answer.[[/CHANGED]]
- [[NEW]]What this question is really asking: Can membership and agreement be different things?[[/NEW]]

#### Discussion Question 3
- **Original item:** Lowe says that “people can be part of many communities.” What might be some of the benefits of belonging to more than one community?
- **Modified student-facing item:** [[CHANGED]]Lowe says people can belong to many communities. What are some good things about that?[[/CHANGED]]
- [[NEW]]What this question is really asking: What can you gain from having more than one group you belong to?[[/NEW]]
