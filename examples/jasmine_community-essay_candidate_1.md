# Tomorrow's Lesson — Jasmine (Grade 7)

## PART A — Teacher Cheat Sheet

### Before the bell
- [[NEW]]Put Jasmine in the front row, on the aisle, with the modified question sheet already on her desk and a fresh copy of the text with paragraph numbers visible.[[/NEW]]
- [[NEW]]Circle 3 places in the text where the author defines community or gives a reason it matters: paragraph 2, paragraph 9, paragraph 11.[[/NEW]]
- [[NEW]]Pre-write three praise notes on a sticky note so you can drop them in the moment: one for annotation, one for a turn-and-talk comment, one for the short response.[[/NEW]]

### Watch for
- [[NEW]]Head down, page not turned, or staring at one paragraph too long when the lesson moves from paragraph 2 to paragraph 3. This often shows up when the text shifts from list-style structure to abstract definition, which is harder for her current reading level. [[/NEW]]
- [[NEW]]Extra restroom request, pencil fiddling, or skipping the question she is supposed to answer during Independent Practice. That fits her pattern of frustration → avoidance. [[/NEW]]
- [[NEW]]Very short answers like “it means a group” with no text detail, especially on questions about “identity-forming narrative” or “central idea.” That matches her ELA need for literal/inferential support and low writing stamina. [[/NEW]]

### If they disengage
Use this exact 2-step script with a calm voice:

1. [[NEW]]“Pause, plan, proceed. Take one breath with me. Put your finger on paragraph __ and read just the first sentence.”[[/NEW]]
2. [[NEW]]“Now choose: keep reading with me, or take your scheduled break and come back for question __.”[[/NEW]]

Why this works: it uses an IEP-named support already in the plan — [[NEW]]“Reminders to pause, plan, proceed,” “Scheduled breaks,” and “Frequent breaks.”[[/NEW]]

### Praise specifically
- [[NEW]]“You just pulled the exact detail that shows the author’s definition. That’s strong text evidence.”[[/NEW]]
- [[NEW]]“I like how you stayed with the question even when the words were tricky. That’s good re-engagement.”[[/NEW]]
- [[NEW]]“Your answer names the idea and explains it in your own words — that’s what strong comprehension writing looks like.”[[/NEW]]

## PART B — Modified Lesson Plan

### 1) Intro Slide Deck — 5 min
- **Original step:** Guide students through the slide deck to introduce them to the reading lesson and skill focus.
- **Modified step for Jasmine:** [[CHANGED]]Keep her in whole group, but hand her a 3-box preview card before the slides start: Box 1 = “What is community?” Box 2 = “How does the author define it?” Box 3 = “Why does it matter?” Ask her to point to the box that matches each slide as you go.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[NEW]]Goal 3 — ELA: “Independently answer literal and inferential comprehension questions or writing prompts that meet grade-level rubrics, from 50% → 75%.” Benchmarks: “Accurately annotate a given text for the prompt,” “Independently answer literal comprehension questions,” and “Write a claim that accurately answers each part of the question.” Present level: “Struggles with literal/inferential questions verbally and in writing.”[[/NEW]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]3-box preview card; front-row seat; paragraph-numbered text copy.[[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[NEW]]During the first 2 minutes of the intro slides, before reading begins.[[/NEW]]

### 2) Vocabulary preview — built into intro
- **Original step:** Let’s pronounce these words together as a class: aspect, moral, narrative, specific.
- **Modified step for Jasmine:** [[CHANGED]]After the class repeats each word, give Jasmine a one-line meaning strip with a tiny example beside each word: narrative = “story,” moral = “right/wrong,” specific = “clear and exact,” aspect = “part.” Ask her to underline the word that shows up in the article’s definition of community.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[NEW]]Goal 3 — ELA benchmark: “Independently answer literal comprehension questions” and “Write a claim that accurately answers each part of the question.” Present level: “Needs significant adult support to break down vocabulary, sentence structures, key ideas.”[[/NEW]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Meaning strip with 4 words + tiny examples; underline/highlight tool.[[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[NEW]]While the class is practicing the four vocabulary words, before paragraph 1.[[/NEW]]

### 3) Whole Class Reading — Paragraphs 1-2
- **Original step:** Whole class reading with During Reading Question A, B, C.
- **Modified step for Jasmine:** [[CHANGED]]Have Jasmine track paragraphs 1-2 with her finger while you read aloud once. Then pause and ask her only Question A first. If she answers, let her point to the exact sentence that proves it before moving to Question B.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[NEW]]Accommodations: “Repeat directions,” “Copy of teacher’s notes,” and “Reminders to pause, plan, proceed.” Goal 3 benchmark: “Accurately annotate a given text for the prompt.” Present level: “Needs frequent reminders to track text, annotate, reread when independent.”[[/NEW]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Finger-tracking, paragraph-numbered text, teacher note copy, one-question-at-a-time card.[[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[NEW]]During the read-aloud of paragraphs 1-2 and right after paragraph 2.[[/NEW]]

### 4) During Reading Question B — paragraph 2 bullets
- **Original step:** Write: What is the relationship between the claim above and the bulleted list in paragraph 2?
- **Modified step for Jasmine:** [[CHANGED]]Give her a two-part sentence stem: “The claim says ____. The bullets show this by ____.” Have her fill in only one bullet idea first, then add a second only if she is ready.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[NEW]]Goal 3 benchmark: “Write a claim that accurately answers each part of the question.” Present level: “with 1-on-1 support, ~50%, can participate at grade level.”[[/NEW]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Two-part sentence stem; highlight the claim sentence in paragraph 2.[[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[NEW]]Immediately after paragraph 2, before the class turn-and-talk.[[/NEW]]

### 5) During Reading Question C — paragraph 2 turn & talk
- **Original step:** Turn & Talk: Based on the bulleted list, summarize at least three key traits of a community.
- **Modified step for Jasmine:** [[CHANGED]]Let Jasmine stay with a partner in the whole-group setting. Give her a 3-checkbox response card with the traits already named in student-friendly language: “many kinds,” “belongs/doesn’t belong,” “shared story shapes people.” She chooses three and explains one aloud.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[NEW]]Accommodations: “Reminder to remain engaged,” “1:1 check-ins,” and “Sit in the front of the room.” Present level: “Likes participating, open to feedback during 1-on-1 check-ins, warm, gets along with peers.”[[/NEW]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]3-checkbox card; partner talk script; front-row placement.[[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[NEW]]During the class turn-and-talk after paragraph 2.[[/NEW]]

### 6) Partner Reading — Paragraphs 3-7
- **Original step:** Partner reading with During Reading Questions A and B.
- **Modified step for Jasmine:** [[CHANGED]]Assign Jasmine the first short section to read silently, then have the teacher or partner read aloud the harder sentence in paragraph 4. Ask her to stop after paragraph 5 and jot one word for how Lowe feels about Newcastle.[[/CHANGED]]

- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Stop-and-jot box after paragraph 5; teacher read-aloud for the densest sentence; notes copy.[[/NEW]]

### 7) During Reading Question A — paragraph 3
- **Original step:** Write: How does Lowe’s example of belonging to the Newcastle community support his definition?

### 8) During Reading Question B — paragraph 7 turn & talk

- **Modified step for Jasmine:** [[CHANGED]]Let Jasmine choose from 2 community examples she already knows from school, family, or a club, instead of inventing one from scratch. She says whether the group has a shared story, then names one action that shows she belongs.[[/CHANGED]]
- **IEP citation:** [[NEW]]Present level: “Likes participating, open to feedback during 1-on-1 check-ins, warm, gets along with peers.” Goal 3 benchmark: “Write analysis showing how evidence supports the claim.”[[/NEW]]
- **Exact materials swap or scaffold:** [[NEW]]Choice of 2 familiar communities; mini chart: “shared story / action I do.”[[/NEW]]
- **When:** [[NEW]]During the partner turn-and-talk after paragraph 7.[[/NEW]]

### 9) Whole Class Reading — Paragraph 8
