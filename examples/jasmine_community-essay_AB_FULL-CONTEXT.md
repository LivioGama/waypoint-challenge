# Tomorrow's Lesson — Jasmine (Grade 7)

## PART A — Teacher Cheat Sheet

### Before the bell
- [[NEW]]Put Jasmine’s modified question sheet, graphic organizer, and pencil on her desk before students enter.[[/NEW]]
- [[NEW]]Circle 3 places in the text for quick check-ins: paragraph 2 bullets, paragraph 9 definition, and paragraph 11 social change sentence.[[/NEW]]
- [[NEW]]Write one short reminder on a sticky note: “Pause → plan → proceed.”[[/NEW]]

### Watch for
- [[NEW]]Head down, staring at the page, or repeated restroom requests right after the paragraph 2 bullet list or the short response prompt.[[/NEW]]
- [[NEW]]Jasmine answering with a very short “I don’t know” after dense language like “identity-forming narrative,” “normative,” or “archetypes.”[[/NEW]]
- [[NEW]]Skipping ahead or freezing during Independent Practice MCQ #4 or the short response, especially if she has not used the organizer yet.[[/NEW]]

### If they disengage
Use this exact 2-step script with a calm voice:
1. [[NEW]]“Pause. Let’s plan the next 1 step together. Point to the sentence or question you’re on.”[[/NEW]]
2. [[NEW]]“Choose one: 3 deep breaths, a 1-minute movement break at your seat, or the fidget. Then we’ll come back and do the next question together.”[[/NEW]]

[[NEW]]Why this works for Jasmine: Goal 1 — Counseling / Self-regulation says she should “use a taught calming strategy (deep breathing, movement break, fidget tool, sensory tool, grounding) to calm and re-engage.”[[/NEW]]

### Praise specifically
- [[NEW]]“You found the exact sentence that explains the central idea — that’s strong text work.”[[/NEW]]
- [[NEW]]“You kept going after a hard word and used the note sheet to figure it out — that shows real stamina.”[[/NEW]]
- [[NEW]]“You explained your thinking clearly with evidence from the text — that matches what good readers do.”[[/NEW]]

## PART B — Modified Lesson Plan

### 1) Intro Slide Deck — 5 min

- Original step: Introduce the reading lesson and skill focus.
- Modified step for Jasmine: [[CHANGED]]Keep Jasmine in whole-group for the full intro. Before the lesson starts, give her a 20-second heads-up: “Today we’re looking for the author’s main idea and the details that support it.” Point her to the title, the purpose for reading, and the word “community.” Ask her to underline “community” once on her copy.[[/CHANGED]]
- IEP citation: [[NEW]]Goal 3 — ELA: “Independently answer literal and inferential comprehension questions or writing prompts that meet grade-level rubrics, from 50% → 75%.” Also supports Present Level ELA: “Needs significant adult support to break down vocabulary, sentence structures, key ideas, and to organize thinking.”[[/NEW]]
- Exact materials swap or scaffold: [[NEW]]Title box with 1 sentence frame: “This text is mostly about ______.”[[/NEW]]

### 2) Vocabulary warm-up — 1–2 min within intro
- Original step: Pronounce vocabulary together as a class.
- Modified step for Jasmine: [[CHANGED]]After the class repeats the words, hand Jasmine a tiny margin note card with plain-language meanings for “aspect,” “moral,” “narrative,” and “specific.” Ask her to circle “narrative” because it shows up in the main definition.[[/CHANGED]]
- IEP citation: [[NEW]]Goal 3 — ELA benchmark: “Independently answer literal comprehension questions (main idea, setting, plot).” Present Level ELA: “Decodes most grade-level words, adequate fluency. Struggles with literal/inferential questions verbally and in writing.”[[/NEW]]
- Exact materials swap or scaffold: [[NEW]]Mini vocab card with 4 short definitions and one example each.[[/NEW]]

### 3) Whole Class Reading — Paragraphs 1–2 — 6 min
- Original step: Read paragraphs 1–2 and answer question A; discuss the bulleted list.
- Modified step for Jasmine: [[CHANGED]]Keep her in the whole-group read-aloud. When you reach paragraph 2, stop and have Jasmine put a box around the sentence that starts “Here are the things that I think a definition of community must be able to explain…” Then ask her to choose just one bullet from the list and paraphrase it in 8 words or fewer with a partner.[[/CHANGED]]
- IEP citation: [[NEW]]Goal 3 — ELA benchmark: “Accurately annotate a given text for the prompt — labeled annotation focus, supporting detail, etc.” Accommodation: “Repeat directions.”[[/NEW]]
- Exact materials swap or scaffold: [[NEW]]Paragraph 2 mini box with 3 checkboxes: “different types,” “positive feelings,” “in/out group.”[[/NEW]]
- Exact moment: [[NEW]]Right after reading paragraph 2 and before the class discussion question.[[/NEW]]

### 4) During Reading Question A — 2 min
- Original step: Think & Share: What claim does Lowe make about the word “community” in paragraph 1?
- Modified step for Jasmine: [[CHANGED]]Give Jasmine the question with two answer choices already written as sentence starters: “The word ‘community’ is…” and “People do not always clearly explain it because…” She chooses one and says it to a partner before sharing aloud.[[/CHANGED]]
- IEP citation: [[NEW]]Goal 3 — ELA benchmark: “Independently answer literal comprehension questions.” Present Level ELA: “Benefits immensely from small group / 1:1, but chooses whole group 80% of the time.”[[/NEW]]
- Exact materials swap or scaffold: [[NEW]]Two sentence starters on a half-sheet.[[/NEW]]

### 5) Partner Reading — Paragraphs 3–7 — 8 min
- Original step: Read with a partner and answer question A; turn and talk about a community.
- Modified step for Jasmine: [[CHANGED]]Keep Jasmine with a strong partner in the room. Give her a 3-box note catcher: “community name,” “shared story,” “one detail from the text.” After paragraph 5 or 6, pause her for a 15-second check-in: ask her to point to the line that shows the author belongs to Newcastle.[[/CHANGED]]
- IEP citation: [[NEW]]Goal 3 — ELA benchmark: “Independently find effective pieces of textual evidence to support the claim.” Accommodation: “1:1 check-ins,” “Copy of teacher’s notes.”[[/NEW]]
- Exact materials swap or scaffold: [[NEW]]3-box note catcher with one example already started: “Newcastle = shared city story.”[[/NEW]]

### 6) Turn & Talk in Paragraph 6 — 2 min
- Original step: Share how one community fits Lowe’s definition.
- Modified step for Jasmine: [[CHANGED]]Give Jasmine a choice board with 3 communities she can talk about: family, team, school, or neighborhood. She picks one and answers with the frame: “In my community, the shared story is ______.”[[/CHANGED]]
- IEP citation: [[NEW]]Goal 3 — ELA present level: “Likes participating, open to feedback during 1-on-1 check-ins, warm, gets along with peers.”[[/NEW]]
- Exact materials swap or scaffold: [[NEW]]Choice board plus one sentence frame.[[/NEW]]

### 7) Whole Class Reading — Paragraph 8 — 5 min
- Original step: Read paragraph 8 and answer why the author includes another bulleted list.
- Modified step for Jasmine: [[CHANGED]]Before the class answers, ask Jasmine to put a star next to the sentence that says the definition is being tested against the six criteria. Then have her say, “He is checking if his definition works.”[[/CHANGED]]
- IEP citation: [[NEW]]Goal 3 — ELA benchmark: “Write analysis showing how evidence supports the claim.” Present Level ELA: “Struggles with literal/inferential questions verbally and in writing.”[[/NEW]]
- Exact materials swap or scaffold: [[NEW]]Star-and-say response strip.[[/NEW]]

### 8) Partner Reading — Paragraphs 8–9 — 4 min
- Original step: Write why Lowe thinks the definition works.
- Modified step for Jasmine: [[CHANGED]]Have Jasmine complete only 2 of the 3 reasons on the partner sheet, but make them specific. She must copy one phrase from paragraph 8 and explain it in her own words with a sentence frame.[[/CHANGED]]
- IEP citation: [[NEW]]Goal 3 — ELA benchmark: “Independently find effective pieces of textual evidence to support the claim.” Accommodation: “Reference sheets, graphic organizers, and checklists.”[[/NEW]]
- Exact materials swap or scaffold: [[NEW]]Two-reason response box with sentence frames.[[/NEW]]

### 9) Whole Class Reading — Paragraphs 10–11 — 5 min
- Original step: Find evidence that answers both questions in the title.
- Modified step for Jasmine: [[CHANGED]]Tell Jasmine ahead of time that the answer is in two spots. Have her highlight paragraph 9 for the definition and paragraph 11 for why it matters. Ask her to write one quick note in the margin: “what community is” and “why it matters.”[[/CHANGED]]
- IEP citation: [[NEW]]Goal 3 — ELA benchmark: “Accurately annotate a given text for the prompt — labeled annotation focus, supporting detail, etc.”[[/NEW]]
- Exact materials swap or scaffold: [[NEW]]Two-color highlight code: yellow = what community is, blue = why it matters.[[/NEW]]

### 10) Independent Practice MCQ #1–4 — 5 min
- Original step: Students answer four multiple-choice questions.
- Modified step for Jasmine: [[CHANGED]]Give Jasmine the same questions, but one at a time on a separate half-sheet with one short gloss under each question. Read only the question stem aloud if she wants it. After #2, do a 30-second check: “Show me your evidence line.”[[/CHANGED]]
- IEP citation: [[NEW]]Goal 3 — ELA benchmark: “Independently answer literal comprehension questions” and “Independently find effective pieces of textual evidence to support the claim.” Accommodation: “Reminder to remain engaged,” “1:1 check-ins.”[[/NEW]]
- Exact materials swap or scaffold: [[NEW]]One-at-a-time MCQ sheet with a small evidence box under each item.[[/NEW]]

### 11) Short Response Prompt — 15 min
- Original step: Students write the response to the prompt.
- Modified step for Jasmine: [[CHANGED]]Keep the prompt unchanged, but give her a prefilled organizer with 3 boxes: claim, evidence 1, evidence 2. Before she writes, ask her to say her answer out loud in one sentence. Then she writes using sentence stems and the checklist below.[[/CHANGED]]
- IEP citation: [[NEW]]Goal 3 — ELA benchmark: “Write a claim that accurately answers each part of the question,” “Independently find effective pieces of textual evidence to support the claim,” and “Write analysis showing how evidence supports the claim.”[[/NEW]]
- Exact materials swap or scaffold: [[NEW]]Claim-evidence-analysis organizer plus sentence stems.[[/NEW]]
- Exact moment: [[NEW]]Right when independent writing starts, before pencil touches paper.[[/NEW]]

### 12) Student-Led Discussion — 5 min
- Original step: Discuss the questions with a partner and record both answers.
- Modified step for Jasmine: [[CHANGED]]Keep Jasmine with her partner in the main room. Give her the first question only at the start. After she answers, add the second, then the third. Let her use her notes and one follow-up prompt from the teacher if she stalls.[[/CHANGED]]
- IEP citation: [[NEW]]Goal 1 — Counseling / Self-regulation: “Use a taught calming strategy… to calm and re-engage.” Goal 3 — ELA present level: “Loves to help others, motivated by specific positive praise, enjoys peer talk.”[[/NEW]]
- Exact materials swap or scaffold: [[NEW]]One-question-at-a-time discussion card with answer lines.[[/NEW]]

### 5) Independent Practice MCQ — Jasmine version

**Directions:** Answer the multiple choice questions for “What is ‘community’ and why is it important?”

#### 1. Which best describes the author’s main purpose in writing the article? [RI.6]  
A. to illustrate how important it is to be part of multiple communities  
B. to demonstrate the way community has shaped the author's life  
C. to describe the life of a person who lives in Newcastle  
D. to give a clear and useful definition of community

#### 2. Which piece of evidence best illustrates the central idea of the article? [RI.1]

D. “Social change requires that we rewrite our communal narratives.” (Paragraph 11)

A. bored  
B. insulted  
C. motivated  
D. recognized  
[[NEW]]What this question is really asking: What does “slighted” mean here?[[/NEW]]

#### 4. How do paragraphs 5–7 help the reader understand the author’s central idea? [RI.2]
