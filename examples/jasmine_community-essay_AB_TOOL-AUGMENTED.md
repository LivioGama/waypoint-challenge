# Tomorrow's Lesson — Jasmine (Grade 7)

## Part A — Teacher Cheat Sheet

### Before the bell
- [[NEW]]Put Jasmine’s modified question sheet on her desk **before** the Intro Slide Deck starts. [[/NEW]] This is a 60-second prep and keeps her in whole group, which matches her preference for whole-group learning.
- [[NEW]]Write these 3 words on the board or a sticky note at her seat: **community, identity, narrative**. [[/NEW]] She is working on grade-level informational text, but her present level shows vocabulary and inferential questions are a hard spot; seeing the words first lowers the load without lowering the task.
- [[NEW]]Keep a one-page **“pause, plan, proceed”** card and a fidget within reach at her seat. [[/NEW]] Her self-regulation goal says she should use a taught calming strategy, and her behavioral profile says frustration often turns into withdrawal.

### Watch for
- [[NEW]]Head down, looking away during the first definition paragraph or the bullets listing what a definition of community must explain. [[/NEW]] That’s a likely “this is too much text” moment for her, especially with the dense opening vocabulary.
- [[NEW]]Repeated restroom requests right after the long bullet list in Paragraph 2 or during Independent Practice MCQs. [[/NEW]] Her pattern is frustration → avoidance, and the lesson has a lot of text-heavy thinking in those spots.
- [[NEW]]Fast, shallow answers like “I don’t know” when asked to explain the phrase “identity-forming narrative.” [[/NEW]] Her ELA baseline says literal and inferential questions are hard verbally and in writing.

### If they disengage
Use this exact 2-step recovery script:
1. [[NEW]]“Jasmine, pause, plan, proceed — take one deep breath and touch the fidget.”[[/NEW]]
2. [[NEW]]“Now look at just the highlighted sentence and tell me which word is the key word: community, identity, or narrative.”[[/NEW]]

[[NEW]]Why this fits her: the self-regulation goal says, **“Use a taught calming strategy (deep breathing, movement break, fidget tool, sensory tool, grounding) to calm and re-engage 4 of 5 opportunities.”**[[/NEW]]

### Praise specifically
- “[[NEW]]Jasmine, you found the sentence that tells what community means — that’s exactly how strong readers track the central idea.[[/NEW]]”
- “[[NEW]]You used the text to answer that question, not just your memory. That’s real grade 7 reading work.[[/NEW]]”
